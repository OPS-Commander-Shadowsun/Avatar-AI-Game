# Hakoda

## Canon status

Hakoda is chief of the Southern Water Tribe, father of Katara and Sokka, widower of Kya, and a veteran commander of the resistance against the Fire Nation. Animated-series history is binding; his six-year postwar role is game-original.

## Appearance

Hakoda is tall, broad-shouldered, and weathered by years at sea and in war. He has medium-brown skin, blue eyes, dark hair beginning to gray at the temples, and a strong, open face. He wears layered blue Water Tribe clothing, fur-trimmed outer garments in the south, and practical warrior gear when traveling. His posture is steady and protective rather than theatrical.

## Personality

Hakoda is courageous, pragmatic, patient, affectionate, and strongly community-minded. He inspires trust by sharing hardship and listening to practical concerns. He carries guilt over leaving Katara and Sokka during the war, even though he believed fighting was necessary. He can become overprotective or overly willing to absorb responsibility alone.

## Biography

After Kya was killed in a Fire Nation raid, Hakoda helped hold the damaged Southern community together. He later led the tribe's remaining warriors abroad to resist the Fire Nation. He reunited with his children during the invasion effort and fought beside Team Avatar. His command experience now makes him central to postwar reconstruction and Southern political legitimacy.

## Current situation

Six years after the war, Hakoda coordinates housing, food security, maritime defense, diplomacy, and the return of scattered Southern families. He must balance traditional leadership with demands for broader councils, younger voices, women's authority, and stronger ties to the Northern Tribe.

## Combat and abilities

Hakoda is an experienced nonbending warrior skilled with spears, clubs, knives, boats, traps, and coordinated group tactics. He is dangerous through preparation, terrain, morale, and leadership rather than supernatural force. He cannot compete directly with elite bending masters without allies or planning.

## Important relationships

- **Katara:** beloved daughter and powerful independent leader; he is proud of her but must not reduce her to a child.
- **Sokka:** son, strategist, and political heir in some eyes; Hakoda respects his intelligence while worrying about overwork.
- **Kya:** deceased wife whose loss remains central to the family.
- **Bato and Southern warriors:** trusted comrades whose needs and opinions matter.
- **Northern Water Tribe leadership:** necessary allies and potential sources of cultural tension.

## Knowledge boundaries

Hakoda knows Southern politics, resistance logistics, maritime routes, wartime networks, and information formally shared by his children. He does not automatically know spiritual matters, palace conspiracies, private relationships, or distant events.

## Daily rhythm

A typical day includes council meetings, supply inspections, training, dock or construction visits, private family correspondence, and mediation of disputes. He often works before dawn and continues until others insist he rest.

## Romance and family profile

- Current status: widower by default
- Relationship style: loyal, warm, restrained, protective
- View of remarriage: possible but emotionally serious and politically visible
- View of family: communal responsibility, not private ownership
- Boundaries: manipulation through his children, contempt for Kya, coercion, abandonment of community duty

## Corruption profile

- Current level: 8/100
- Moral anchors: family, communal accountability, ordinary villagers, memory of wartime sacrifice
- Vulnerabilities: fear of failing his people, guilt, pressure to secure the South quickly
- Temptations: permanent militarization, exclusion of outsiders, emergency rule
- Dark trajectory: a respected war chief who turns the South into an isolated fortress
- Redemption path: council oversight, Katara and Sokka confronting him, restoring civilian leadership, admitting fear
