# Main Character Bible

This directory contains the fully expanded main-character profiles for the postwar simulation. Profiles define campaign-start defaults; persistent game state overrides those defaults once play begins.

## Part 1 profiles

- [Aang](Aang.md) — Avatar, last known airbender, mediator and spiritual guardian
- [Katara](Katara.md) — Southern Water Tribe master, healer, teacher and humanitarian leader
- [Sokka](Sokka.md) — strategist, inventor, warrior and Southern envoy
- [Fire Lord Zuko](Zuko.md) — Fire Nation ruler, reformer and firebending master
- [Toph Beifong](Toph.md) — earthbending master and inventor of metalbending

## Part 2 profiles

- [Azula](Azula.md) — former princess, blue-fire prodigy and unresolved postwar political figure

## Required profile sections

Every major-character file should define:

- canon status and campaign age;
- detailed physical appearance, clothing, voice and body language;
- personality, values, speech and mannerisms;
- relevant animated-series biography;
- six-year postwar position;
- bending, combat and non-combat abilities;
- limitations and credible counters;
- meaningful relationships;
- adult romance and family profile;
- daily patterns and obligations;
- knowledge boundaries;
- current goals, fears and unresolved wounds;
- corruption level, warning signs, dark trajectory and redemption path;
- prohibited characterization jumps;
- AI portrayal rules;
- quest hooks.

## Runtime loading

Load only the profiles of characters active in or directly relevant to the current scene. For scenes involving politics or family conflict, load every directly affected profile rather than relying on one character's description of another.

Recommended examples:

- Fire Nation throne crisis: Zuko, Azula, Ozai, Iroh and Mai.
- Kyoshi Warrior mission: Suki and Ty Lee, plus the relevant location and faction files.
- Team Avatar reunion: all attending main-character profiles and current relationship state.
- Azula recovery scene: Azula plus the profiles of actual participants; never let another file replace her own autonomy.

## Continuity rules

- The original animated series is historical canon.
- *The Legend of Korra* and post-series comics are excluded.
- All listed central characters are adults at campaign start.
- Attraction never overrides autonomy, existing commitments, obligations or personality.
- Corruption is gradual and requires credible causes, choices and consequences.
- Redemption does not erase accountability or guarantee forgiveness.
- Character knowledge is limited to witnessed, learned or reasonably inferred information.
- Nonbenders remain constrained by human range, terrain, equipment, fatigue and injury.
- Powerful characters cannot solve every problem and should retain independent schedules and priorities.
