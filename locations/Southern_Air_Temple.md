# Southern Air Temple

## Overview

The Southern Air Temple is a high-altitude Air Nomad complex of towers, courtyards, meditation chambers, sanctuaries, statues, archives, animal spaces, and damaged residential areas. It is sacred ground, a site of genocide, and one of the most emotionally significant places in the world for Aang.

Six years after the war, the temple remains only partially stabilized. Preservation teams, spiritual visitors, historians, scavengers, opportunists, and officials disagree over who has the right to enter, restore, study, or protect it.

## Geography and climate

The temple stands high in remote mountains accessible by flying bison, glider, difficult climbing routes, or carefully organized expeditions. Thin air, severe winds, sudden storms, cold nights, unstable paths, and falling hazards make ordinary travel dangerous.

## Architecture

The complex uses tall towers, curved roofs, open-air bridges, courtyards, circular rooms, meditation spaces, hidden passages, statuary, and architecture designed around air movement. Some structures remain intact; others contain collapsed floors, fire damage, weather erosion, or unexploded remnants of battle.

## Sacred character

The temple is not an abandoned dungeon. It is a memorial site, cultural archive, former home, and place of spiritual practice. Human remains, damaged artifacts, murals, statues, and domestic spaces must be treated with respect.

## Custodianship

Aang is the primary living cultural authority by default, but he cannot personally guard every chamber. Trusted allies, scholars, preservation workers, White Lotus contacts, and local mountain communities may help. No government automatically owns the temple.

## Resources and services

There are no ordinary markets or permanent public inns. Expeditions must bring food, climbing gear, medicine, fuel, tools, and shelter. Limited restored quarters may exist for approved custodians and visitors.

## Associated characters and factions

- Aang: cultural survivor and spiritual custodian
- Appa and Momo: familiar with Air Nomad spaces
- Team Avatar Network
- White Lotus preservation contacts
- Air-associated pilgrims and scholars, none of whom are airbenders by default
- relic smugglers and fraudulent spiritual groups

## Daily rhythm

Permanent activity is limited. A restoration day may include safety inspection, meditation, cataloging, structural work, animal care, and controlled exploration. Silence and weather often dominate the site.

## Travel connections

Travel connects to remote mountain routes, temporary base camps, and distant Earth Kingdom settlements. Access requires preparation and usually permission. Appa is not public transport and Aang may refuse requests.

## Reputation tracks

Track Aang’s trust, custodian approval, scholarly credibility, spiritual respect, and desecration notoriety separately.

## Current tensions

- governments claiming legal ownership of recovered artifacts;
- looting and counterfeit relics;
- disagreement over opening the site to pilgrims;
- unsafe amateur restoration;
- Aang’s grief conflicting with public expectations;
- false claims of surviving Air Nomad lineages;
- possible spirit responses to disturbance.
