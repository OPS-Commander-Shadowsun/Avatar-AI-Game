# Northern Air Temple

## Overview

The Northern Air Temple is an ancient Air Nomad complex adapted during the war by displaced Earth Kingdom refugees and the Mechanist. It combines sacred Air Nomad architecture with workshops, glider platforms, residences, mechanical systems, and scars from Fire Nation attack.

Six years after the war, the temple remains one of the clearest examples of cultural preservation colliding with survival-driven adaptation. Its residents are not Air Nomads by default, and its inventions do not grant airbending.

## Geography and climate

The temple stands in high mountains reached through narrow paths, lifts, gliders, flying animals, or difficult climbing routes. Wind, altitude, cold, rockfall, and mechanical failure are constant hazards.

## Architecture

Original towers, open chambers, air channels, courtyards, statues, sanctuaries, and glider spaces coexist with pipes, workshops, platforms, lifts, storage rooms, and refugee housing. Some modifications preserve life; others damaged or obscured sacred features.

## Population and culture

Residents may include refugee families, mechanics, glider pilots, craftspeople, students, farmers working limited terraces, healers, and preservation workers. Their culture blends Earth Kingdom backgrounds, wartime communal survival, engineering practice, and growing respect for Air Nomad heritage.

## Government and authority

The settlement is governed through community leadership, workshop authority, family representatives, and custodial agreements involving Aang. The Mechanist has major technical influence but should not possess unchecked political authority.

## Economy

The community depends on engineering, repairs, glider construction, mountain transport, limited agriculture, trade, salvage, and outside supply. Fuel, metal, food, medicine, cloth, and replacement parts are persistent needs.

## Security

Defenses include difficult access, glider scouts, mechanical barriers, community watches, terrain knowledge, and possible allies. The temple is vulnerable to siege, sabotage, fire, supply interruption, and theft of inventions.

## Daily life

Residents maintain machinery, train with gliders, repair structures, teach children, manage supplies, and negotiate access. Wind conditions shape the schedule. Workshop noise and sacred quiet exist in tension.

## Travel connections

Routes connect to nearby mountain settlements, Earth Kingdom roads, supply camps, and aerial paths. Access requires climbing skill, gliders, machinery, or special transport.
