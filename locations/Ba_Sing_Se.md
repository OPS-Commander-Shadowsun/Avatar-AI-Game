# Ba Sing Se

## Overview

Ba Sing Se is the largest known city in the Earth Kingdom and the symbolic center of continental government. Its scale, layered walls, agricultural perimeter, social rings, train routes, bureaucracy, and hidden infrastructure make it less a single settlement than a collection of cities governed under one name.

Six years after the war, Ba Sing Se is rebuilding after Fire Nation occupation, Dai Li manipulation, wartime isolation, refugee influx, and the collapse of carefully maintained public myths. Restoration has reopened institutions, but old habits of secrecy, class privilege, bribery, and administrative denial remain deeply rooted.

## Geography and climate

The city stands behind immense concentric walls surrounded by broad agricultural lands. Roads, canals, rail lines, checkpoints, and wall gates control movement. Summers are warm and crowded; winters can be cold enough to strain poorer districts. Dust, smoke, sewage, and market odors vary sharply by ring.

## Districts

### Agrarian Zone

Fields, irrigation systems, granaries, farm villages, military depots, and outer-wall defenses sustain the city. Farmers and laborers are essential but politically weak. Crop failure or damaged transport immediately affects food prices.

### Lower Ring

The Lower Ring houses workers, refugees, small merchants, migrants, performers, criminal networks, cheap inns, crowded housing, workshops, clinics, and informal religious communities. It is culturally diverse and politically volatile.

### Middle Ring

The Middle Ring contains established merchants, schools, guild offices, respectable housing, restaurants, theaters, administrative services, and many professional households. It is the city’s commercial bridge between poverty and privilege.

### Upper Ring

The Upper Ring contains noble estates, senior officials, elite academies, embassies, gardens, and controlled access to the royal complex. Wealth is visible in space, quiet, servants, security, and political connections.

### Royal Palace District

The palace, ministries, ceremonial grounds, archives, guard compounds, and reception halls form the official center of Earth Kingdom authority. Access depends on rank, invitation, employment, or political leverage.

### Underground network

Catacombs, abandoned tunnels, service passages, crystal caverns, hidden Dai Li facilities, smuggling routes, and sealed wartime spaces exist beneath parts of the city. Maps are incomplete and often deliberately falsified.

## Architecture

Earthbending shaped walls, platforms, roads, terraces, gates, and public buildings. Architecture becomes larger and more ornamented toward the Upper Ring. Lower districts use denser construction, reused materials, shared courtyards, and improvised additions.

## Population and culture

Ba Sing Se contains refugees, rural migrants, nobles, artisans, bureaucrats, soldiers, scholars, performers, traders, criminals, foreign delegations, and families from nearly every Earth Kingdom region. No single local custom represents the whole city.

Class determines clothing, speech, legal treatment, schooling, housing, and freedom of movement. Ring prejudice is common. Refugee neighborhoods preserve regional customs while adapting to urban life.

## Government and politics

Earth King Kuei is the formal sovereign. Ministries, noble families, provincial delegations, palace officials, city guards, guilds, and remnants of Dai Li influence compete beneath the throne.

Political conflicts include:

- civilian oversight of the Dai Li;
- refugee rights and resettlement;
- noble taxation;
- corruption in food and housing distribution;
- decentralization versus royal authority;
- war-crime investigations;
- relations with Fire Nation communities and diplomats.

## Economy

Ba Sing Se consumes enormous quantities of grain, vegetables, tea, coal, textiles, metal, timber, ceramics, medicine, paper, and animal products. Major sectors include administration, transport, construction, hospitality, education, luxury crafts, food processing, security, and illicit trade.

Prices differ sharply by ring. Bribes, permits, guild membership, debt, and family connections often matter as much as coin.

## Security and crime

The city has palace guards, ordinary law officers, private estate security, guild enforcers, local watch groups, and possible reformed Dai Li units. Jurisdiction is confusing and can be exploited.

Common crimes include document forgery, permit fraud, smuggling, extortion, housing scams, illegal detention, bribery, theft, black-market medicine, and political intimidation.

## Daily life

Morning trains and work crews begin before the administrative day. Markets peak by midday. Palace business, guild negotiations, school sessions, deliveries, and construction continue until evening. Nightlife is concentrated in restaurants, theaters, tea houses, gambling rooms, and illegal venues.

Travel between rings may require time, money, paperwork, or inspection. Important news often spreads differently in each ring.

## Travel connections

Ba Sing Se connects to Full Moon Bay, major Earth Kingdom roads, agricultural settlements, military routes, and coastal shipping through overland transfer. Travel can be delayed by permits, damaged infrastructure, banditry, weather, and provincial conflict.
