# Omashu

## Overview

Omashu is a major Earth Kingdom city built across steep stone formations and linked by ramps, bridges, tunnels, lifts, and gravity-driven delivery chutes. It combines ancient earthbending engineering with dense trade, military significance, eccentric civic traditions, and the personal influence of King Bumi.

Six years after the war, Omashu is recovering from occupation damage and renewed regional responsibility. Its citizens are proud of having survived both conquest and liberation, but debate continues over royal authority, rebuilding priorities, veterans, and whether Bumi’s unconventional leadership can produce stable institutions.

## Geography and architecture

The city rises vertically across natural and shaped stone. Movement depends on stairs, earthbent platforms, bridges, ramps, tunnels, and delivery infrastructure. High districts command views and wind exposure; lower areas collect traffic, dust, runoff, and market crowds.

Buildings are carved into or built against stone, with green roofs, courtyards, defensive gates, warehouses, workshops, and public plazas. Earthbenders maintain much of the infrastructure, creating dependence on skilled labor.

## Population and culture

Omashu contains merchants, soldiers, engineers, farmers, artisans, refugees, healers, performers, couriers, and travelers. Civic identity values resilience, improvisation, humor, practical intelligence, and earthbending skill, but not every resident approves of Bumi’s methods.

## Government

King Bumi is the sovereign and dominant political personality. A royal council, city administrators, military officers, guild representatives, and neighborhood leaders handle daily governance. The central political question is how much authority should depend on one extraordinary king.

## Economy

Omashu profits from inland trade, stonework, agriculture, metal goods, engineering, transport services, military supply, and regional markets. The delivery system makes movement efficient but vulnerable to sabotage, maintenance failures, and monopolistic control.

## Security and crime

City guards, earthbending units, palace security, guild watches, and neighborhood defenders share responsibility. Smuggling through tunnels, delivery theft, counterfeit permits, occupation-era weapons, and corruption in reconstruction contracts are common problems.

## Associated characters and factions

- King Bumi: ruler and earthbending master
- Aang: old friend and occasional diplomatic visitor
- White Lotus: discreet influence through Bumi
- Earth Kingdom Royal Administration: formal but not always dominant authority
- local guilds, veterans’ groups, merchant houses, and resistance networks

## Daily life

The city begins early with deliveries, stone maintenance, market setup, and caravan arrivals. Midday traffic is intense. Evening brings taverns, public performances, training yards, rooftop gatherings, and night markets. Sudden route closures can reshape an entire day.

## Travel connections

Omashu connects to southern and central Earth Kingdom roads, farming settlements, mountain passes, and caravan routes. Travel is affected by landslides, bandits, damaged bridges, military checkpoints, and seasonal rain.
