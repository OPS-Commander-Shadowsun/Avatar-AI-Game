# Northern Water Tribe

## Overview

The Northern Water Tribe is a monumental polar city protected by layered ice walls, gates, canals, bridges, watchtowers, and trained waterbenders. It survived the war as a major center of waterbending, healing, scholarship, spirituality, and maritime power, but the memory of Admiral Zhao’s siege remains politically and spiritually significant.

## Geography and climate

The city is built into a polar coastline surrounded by sea ice, mountains, glaciers, and dangerous channels. Waterbenders continually maintain canals, walls, and transport routes. Blizzards, shifting ice, long darkness, and seasonal storms affect trade and defense.

## Architecture and districts

The city uses monumental ice construction, carved interiors, elevated walkways, canals, courtyards, training pools, docks, and ceremonial spaces.

Major areas include:

- palace and council district;
- military harbor;
- residential quarters;
- artisan and market canals;
- healing academies;
- waterbending training grounds;
- outer defensive walls;
- sacred routes leading toward the Spirit Oasis.

## Population and culture

The population includes nobles, warriors, healers, fishers, sailors, craftspeople, students, servants, merchants, spiritual custodians, and visiting Southerners. Northern identity often emphasizes continuity, order, formal training, and survival through discipline.

Traditions are contested. Younger citizens, women trained in combat, Southern visitors, veterans, and reformers challenge older assumptions about gender, class, and authority.

## Government

A chief or royal household governs with councilors, military leaders, senior waterbending masters, healers, and influential families. Formal rank matters. Access to leadership often depends on lineage, reputation, education, or sponsorship.

## Economy

Fishing, whaling substitutes where ethically appropriate, preserved foods, fur, ivory-like animal materials, healing, ice engineering, maritime trade, boatbuilding, luxury crafts, and training support the economy. The North imports timber, metal, grain, tea, medicine ingredients, and manufactured goods.

## Security

The tribe maintains waterbending defenders, naval patrols, gate guards, scouts, healers, and spiritual custodians. Security is strongest near the palace, harbor, and Spirit Oasis. Espionage, smuggling, factional politics, and misuse of sacred access remain concerns.

## Daily life

Waterbenders maintain infrastructure from early morning. Markets, lessons, military drills, fishing expeditions, healing work, and council business continue through the day. Formal meals, ceremonies, and family obligations structure elite life more strongly than in the South.

## Travel connections

Ships and ice routes connect the city to smaller polar settlements, hunting grounds, Southern waters, and international ports. Access can be limited by weather, military alert, diplomatic status, or sacred restrictions.
