# Si Wong Desert

## Overview

The Si Wong Desert is a vast Earth Kingdom wilderness of dunes, stone outcrops, salt flats, hidden ruins, caravan routes, sandbender settlements, rare water sources, and extreme environmental danger. Political borders matter less here than knowledge of wind, stars, animals, wells, and local agreements.

## Environment

Daytime heat, nighttime cold, sandstorms, dehydration, disorientation, unstable dunes, venomous animals, and long distances make travel lethal without preparation. Maps become unreliable as routes and landmarks shift.

## People and culture

Sandbender communities, caravan families, traders, guides, scavengers, nomads, scholars, smugglers, and isolated settlements inhabit the desert. Outsiders often stereotype sandbenders as criminals; local communities distinguish between clans, professions, obligations, and reputations.

## Economy

Trade depends on water, guides, transport animals, salvage, salt, rare minerals, desert plants, glass, textiles, information, and access to safe routes. Water theft and false route information are treated as grave offenses.

## Authority and law

No single government controls the entire desert. Clan agreements, caravan contracts, oasis customs, temporary councils, and armed reputation shape justice. Earth Kingdom officials have authority only where they can maintain presence.

## Travel

Travel requires water planning, shade, navigation, transport, weather awareness, and trusted guides. Sand-sailers and desert-adapted animals may greatly improve speed but require skill and maintenance.

## Reputation tracks

Track individual clan trust, guide reputation, caravan reliability, oasis standing, scholarly reputation, and water-related offenses separately.

## Current tensions

- governments seeking minerals or ruins;
- outsiders stealing water or artifacts;
- feuds between clans over routes;
- smugglers using refugee caravans as cover;
- ecological damage near oases;
- scholars ignoring local ownership of discoveries.

## Quest hooks

- A caravan arrives without its guide and with contradictory memories.

- A buried structure emerges after a sandstorm.
- Stolen sky-bison artifacts are offered at a desert market.
- An Earth Kingdom mining claim threatens a nomadic route.
- A spirit appears only to travelers who are nearly lost.
