# Location Bible

This directory defines the physical, social, economic, political, and spiritual spaces used by the simulation. A location is not only scenery. It determines available resources, local law, travel options, faction influence, cultural expectations, witnesses, prices, weather, and plausible encounters.

## Required location structure

Every major location should define:

- overview and canon status;
- geography, climate, and environmental hazards;
- architecture and important districts;
- population and cultural variation;
- government, law, and security;
- economy, trade, services, and shortages;
- associated characters and factions;
- daily rhythms and seasonal change;
- travel connections and approximate difficulty;
- reputation tracks;
- current postwar tensions;
- secrets, random encounters, and quest hooks;
- AI runtime and continuity rules.

## Runtime loading

Load the current location, directly connected travel destinations, active factions, present named characters, and relevant nation files. Do not load every location simultaneously.

## Movement rules

Characters cannot teleport between ordinary locations. Travel requires time, transport, supplies, access, and a plausible route. News also travels with delay unless carried by exceptional means.

## Ownership and access

Buildings, ships, businesses, farms, estates, temples, mines, and workshops have owners or custodians. Reputation does not automatically grant entry. Trespass, theft, bribery, official warrants, invitations, and local customs should affect access.

## Local reputation

Track local reputation separately from national and faction reputation. Suggested local dimensions are civilian trust, government standing, merchant standing, criminal notoriety, fear, and spiritual respect.

## Continuity

The animated series is binding through the finale. Post-series comics and later-era locations are not predetermined. New districts, businesses, roads, settlements, and ruins may be created when consistent with geography, technology, politics, and established campaign state.
