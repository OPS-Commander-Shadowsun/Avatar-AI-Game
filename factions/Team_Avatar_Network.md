# Team Avatar Network

## Nature

The Team Avatar Network is not a state, army, or formal guild. It is an informal transnational alliance built from the people who fought beside Aang, their trusted associates, local resistance contacts, healers, engineers, diplomats, and community leaders.

## Composition

The inner circle consists of Aang, Katara, Sokka, Toph, Zuko, Suki, Appa, and Momo. Iroh is a senior ally rather than a subordinate. Hakoda, Piandao, Bumi, Pakku, Jeong Jeong, Haru, Teo, the Mechanist, and selected Kyoshi Warriors may be contacted for specialist support.

The wider network contains former resistance members, village leaders, safe-house keepers, transport crews, healers, translators, scouts, and officials who trust at least one member of the inner circle.

## Purpose and activities

- respond to threats governments cannot or will not address;
- mediate international crises;
- investigate war crimes, trafficking, spirit disturbances, and dangerous technology;
- protect civilians and witnesses;
- coordinate specialists across national borders;
- preserve friendships and shared responsibility after the war;
- challenge one another when power, grief, or political duty causes harmful decisions.

## Structure

There is no permanent commander. Leadership follows expertise and circumstance. Aang has unique moral and spiritual authority as Avatar, but cannot issue unlimited orders. Sokka commonly coordinates plans. Katara leads medical or humanitarian work. Zuko commands only where Fire Nation law or military authority applies. Suki commands Kyoshi Warriors. Toph decides how her bending and students are used.

## Joining

There is no public application. A person becomes an affiliate through repeated trustworthy cooperation with one or more members. Inner-circle status requires shared danger, personal trust, demonstrated independence, and acceptance by several existing members. Flattery, fame, romance, or one heroic act are insufficient.

## Advancement and loss of trust

Access grows from local assistance to specialist missions, sensitive information, and strategic consultation. Trust can be lost through coercion, betrayal, exploitation of the Avatar's name, avoidable harm to civilians, or attempts to divide members for personal gain.

## Character affiliations

- **Aang:** central figure and spiritual mediator.
- **Katara:** healer, humanitarian leader, moral challenger.
- **Sokka:** strategist, planner, inventor, logistics coordinator.
- **Toph:** earthbending and metalbending specialist; independent ally.
- **Zuko:** Fire Nation ruler and political partner.
- **Suki:** field commander and security specialist.
- **Iroh:** trusted elder adviser and White Lotus bridge.
- **Hakoda:** Southern military and political ally.
- **Haru:** Earth Kingdom resistance contact.
- **Teo and the Mechanist:** engineering and mobility support.

Azula, Mai, Ty Lee, Ozai, Long Feng, Hama, and June are not members by default. Mai and Ty Lee may cooperate on specific missions. June is a paid specialist unless deeper trust develops.

## Internal tensions

The network disagrees over secrecy, state authority, punishment, nonviolence, surveillance, and personal obligations. These disagreements are a feature, not a malfunction. The group should not behave as a unanimous council controlled by Aang or the player.

## Player interaction

The player can become a contact, affiliate, or trusted specialist. Access may provide urgent missions, introductions, travel help, or personal mentorship. It does not grant immunity, unrestricted entry to palaces, command over named characters, or automatic romantic access.

## AI rules

- Track trust separately with individual members and the network as a whole.
- Do not reveal every member's secrets because one member trusts the player.
- Named members can refuse joint action when duties conflict.
- The network has influence but limited staff, time, money, and legal authority.
