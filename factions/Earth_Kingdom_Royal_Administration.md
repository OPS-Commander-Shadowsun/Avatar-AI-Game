# Earth Kingdom Royal Administration

## Nature

The Earth Kingdom Royal Administration is the civil apparatus formally serving Earth King Kuei and coordinating the enormous, decentralized Earth Kingdom. It includes the royal court, ministries, provincial offices, tax and land registries, diplomatic staff, city administrators, judges, engineers, and messengers.

## Composition and work

The administration handles petitions, taxation, roads, agriculture, refugee settlement, trade, provincial disputes, reconstruction, city charters, courts, records, and relations with autonomous or semi-autonomous regions. Its reach is uneven; local rulers, nobles, military commanders, villages, and guilds may possess more practical power than Ba Sing Se.

## Structure

- Earth King;
- royal advisers and ministers;
- central ministries;
- provincial governors, magistrates, and city councils;
- inspectors, clerks, engineers, judges, and tax officials;
- liaison offices for local rulers, military forces, and foreign governments.

The Dai Li are not identical to the civil administration. Any postwar security role must be legally defined and supervised.

## Joining

Civil servants may enter through education, examinations, apprenticeship, local nomination, noble patronage, technical expertise, or wartime service. Senior positions require experience, political trust, and knowledge of regional law.

The player may join as clerk, surveyor, engineer, investigator, guard, interpreter, envoy, magistrate's aide, or special commissioner. Advancement requires completed service, reputation, administrative competence, and support from more than one patron.

## Character affiliations

- **Earth King Kuei:** sovereign and head of the lawful administration.
- **Long Feng:** former Grand Secretariat who secretly dominated the state; removed from lawful authority by default.
- **Bumi:** king of Omashu and powerful regional ruler, allied with but not ordinarily subordinate in daily affairs.
- **Toph:** influential Earth Kingdom noble by birth and independent master; no default office.
- **Haru:** local resistance hero and possible community representative.
- **Aang:** Avatar and mediator, not an Earth Kingdom official.
- **Sokka and Katara:** foreign allies or advisers when formally invited.
- **Zuko:** foreign head of state and negotiation counterpart.

## Internal blocs

Royal centralizers, provincial autonomists, noble families, anti-corruption reformers, former Dai Li clients, refugee advocates, conservative bureaucrats, merchants, military interests, and local officials defending practical independence.

## Membership consequences

Officials gain salary, records, travel orders, legal authority, and access to state resources. They owe accurate reporting, lawful conduct, financial accountability, and service to assigned communities. Bribery, falsifying records, forced displacement, collaboration with secret police, and theft of relief supplies can lead to dismissal or trial.

## Player interaction

Faction missions may involve audits, missing taxes, corrupt governors, refugee land claims, road repair, border negotiations, forged decrees, or Dai Li infiltration. Rank grants authority only within jurisdiction and written mandate.

## AI rules

- The Earth Kingdom is too large for instant central control.
- Messages, taxes, troops, and investigations require travel time.
- Kuei's orders may be delayed, distorted, resisted, or ignored.
- Local law and custom vary widely.
- Bureaucrats should include honest professionals as well as corrupt officials.
