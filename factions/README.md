# Faction Bible

This directory defines the major political, military, spiritual, criminal, and social organizations active six years after the Hundred Year War.

## Runtime purpose

Faction files explain:

- what the organization is made of;
- what it does in daily life and during crises;
- how authority, ranks, resources, and internal factions work;
- how a player or NPC may join, advance, resign, be expelled, or become an enemy;
- which established characters belong to, lead, oppose, supervise, or influence it;
- what information ordinary members and leaders can realistically access;
- how reputation with the faction affects missions, prices, protection, travel, and political consequences.

## Membership rules

Membership is never granted by a single dialogue choice when the organization controls sensitive authority. Recruitment may require sponsorship, tests, service, residence, background checks, oaths, training, or political appointment. A character may be allied with a faction without being a formal member.

A character can belong to several organizations when duties do not directly conflict. Conflicting loyalties must create decisions and consequences rather than being ignored.

## Core faction states

Use one of these states for the player and relevant NPCs:

- **Unknown:** the faction has no meaningful record of the person.
- **Observed:** the faction is gathering information or evaluating them.
- **Contact:** trusted enough for limited conversation or low-risk work.
- **Affiliate:** assists the faction but has no internal authority.
- **Member:** formally accepted and subject to rules.
- **Officer:** holds command, administrative, instructional, or investigative authority.
- **Leader:** shapes strategy and controls major resources.
- **Suspended:** temporarily stripped of access while investigated.
- **Expelled:** removed and usually barred from rejoining.
- **Hostile:** treated as an active threat.

## Reputation scale

Faction reputation is separate from formal rank:

- -100 to -61: hunted enemy
- -60 to -21: hostile or distrusted
- -20 to 20: unknown, mixed, or neutral
- 21 to 60: respected ally
- 61 to 90: trusted insider
- 91 to 100: legendary service, without automatic immunity from law

## Continuity rules

- Animated-series membership and history are binding.
- Postwar organizational reforms are game-original defaults.
- Later-media institutions and outcomes are not imported.
- Factions are not single personalities. Every organization contains reformers, opportunists, traditionalists, career professionals, idealists, and dissenters.
- Named characters retain autonomy and may resign, split, challenge leadership, or refuse faction orders when credible.
