# Kyoshi Warriors

## Nature

The Kyoshi Warriors are a disciplined martial order rooted in Kyoshi Island's history, community defense, and the legacy of Avatar Kyoshi. They are nonbending specialists by tradition, though membership is defined by training and service rather than hostility toward benders.

## Composition

The order includes field warriors, trainees, instructors, armorers, healers, scouts, quartermasters, local elders, and trusted support staff. Units are small, mobile, and accustomed to coordinated action.

## Activities

- defend Kyoshi Island and its communities;
- escort diplomats, refugees, witnesses, and vulnerable travelers;
- conduct reconnaissance, rescue, and prisoner-extraction missions;
- train local defenders and nonbending martial artists;
- investigate impersonators or misuse of Kyoshi symbols;
- support peacekeeping only where leadership and accountability are clear.

## Structure

Suki is the default senior field commander. Local elders and experienced instructors retain authority over tradition, island defense, and recruitment. Mission command follows appointment and expertise. The order is not Suki's private army.

## Joining

Candidates normally require residence, sponsorship, or a sustained record of service to Kyoshi communities. Outsiders may be admitted, but not merely because they admire the uniform.

Requirements typically include:

- physical and weapons assessment;
- discipline and teamwork training;
- instruction in Kyoshi history and ethics;
- conduct review and consent to the order's rules;
- probationary community service;
- acceptance by instructors and unit peers.

Bending is not an automatic disqualification, but a bender must learn the order's nonbending methods and cannot use elemental power to bypass training.

## Ranks

- trainee;
- probationary warrior;
- full warrior;
- senior warrior or specialist;
- instructor;
- unit leader;
- commander.

## Character affiliations

- **Suki:** commander, instructor, and principal public representative.
- **Ty Lee:** full member by default; chi-blocking and acrobatics specialist.
- **Sokka:** trained ally and trusted partner, not automatically a formal member.
- **Aang, Katara, Toph, and Zuko:** respected allies without command authority.
- **Avatar Kyoshi:** historical and spiritual inspiration, not a source of automatic daily orders.

## Discipline and expulsion

Members can be suspended for endangering civilians, using the uniform for private intimidation, disobeying lawful mission limits, or concealing conflicts of interest. Serious abuse, extortion, political assassination, or betrayal can cause expulsion.

## Internal tensions

Expansion beyond Kyoshi Island creates debate over neutrality, payment, foreign deployments, mixed-gender recruitment, bender membership, and whether the order risks becoming an international police force.

## Player interaction

The player may train as an affiliate, serve alongside a unit, or pursue formal membership. High reputation grants trust, equipment access, and assignments but not command over Suki, Ty Lee, or independent units.

## AI rules

- Treat each warrior as an individual.
- The uniform does not erase personality or dissent.
- Membership requires cultural education and service.
- The order prioritizes protection and controlled force over spectacle.
- Cross-border operations must create political consequences.
