# Dai Li

## Nature

The Dai Li are an elite Earth Kingdom security and intelligence organization originally created to preserve Ba Sing Se's cultural order. By the end of the war they had become a secretive political police force shaped by surveillance, coercion, censorship, and loyalty to institutional control rather than lawful government.

## Composition

The organization includes earthbending agents, handlers, interrogators, informants, archivists, prison staff, surveillance officers, and officials embedded in city administration. Elite agents are trained in stone-glove techniques, wall and tunnel movement, silent coordination, restraint, and urban pursuit.

## Activities

- protect Ba Sing Se from espionage, sabotage, and organized violence;
- gather intelligence through informants and covert observation;
- secure sensitive government facilities and underground routes;
- investigate threats to the Earth King;
- suppress information and manipulate witnesses when corrupt leadership prevails;
- maintain hidden detention, record, and communication systems.

## Structure

The Dai Li use compartmentalized cells, mission commanders, handlers, and a central directorate. Long Feng formerly controlled the organization. After Azula's seizure of power and the liberation of Ba Sing Se, the surviving structure is divided among detained loyalists, hidden cells, opportunists, and reform-minded agents willing to submit to oversight.

## Joining

Public recruitment is rare. Candidates are drawn from highly skilled earthbenders, security officials, military scouts, bureaucratic investigators, and families with established ties.

Typical requirements include:

- advanced earthbending and urban mobility;
- psychological screening and background investigation;
- secrecy oath;
- surveillance and interrogation training;
- probation under a handler;
- demonstrated loyalty to the recognized command structure.

A reformed Dai Li should require legal accountability and independent oversight. A covert remnant may recruit through blackmail, ideology, family pressure, or promises of restored order.

## Ranks

- informant or external asset;
- probationary agent;
- field agent;
- senior agent;
- handler or cell leader;
- directorate officer;
- director.

## Character affiliations

- **Long Feng:** former director and principal architect of the corrupt wartime system.
- **Earth King Kuei:** lawful sovereign whom the Dai Li claimed to protect while deceiving him; now responsible for reform or dissolution.
- **Azula:** briefly commanded Dai Li agents after turning them against Long Feng; not a lawful member by default.
- **Aang, Katara, Sokka, Toph, Zuko, and Iroh:** former enemies of the corrupt organization; may support controlled reform but do not trust it automatically.

## Internal factions

- loyalists to Long Feng;
- royal reformists accepting civilian oversight;
- institutional traditionalists who value order above rulers;
- criminal splinter cells selling intelligence and coercive services;
- frightened lower-level staff seeking amnesty.

## Leaving and expulsion

Formal resignation is difficult because members hold sensitive knowledge. A lawful reform process may use monitored retirement, testimony agreements, or reassignment. Corrupt cells may treat defection as betrayal and target former members.

## Player interaction

The player may investigate, infiltrate, expose, reform, join, or oppose a Dai Li branch. Formal membership should require substantial Earth Kingdom ties and scrutiny. Cooperation with one cell does not grant access to all cells.

## AI rules

- Do not portray every Dai Li agent as identical or fanatically loyal.
- Stone gloves and wall movement require earth and practiced technique.
- Intelligence must come from sources, surveillance, records, or interrogation—not omniscience.
- Reform must address detention, coercion, censorship, and civilian oversight.
- Long Feng remains dangerous through information and networks even without public office.
