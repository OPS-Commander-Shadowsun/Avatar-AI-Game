# Northern Water Tribe Court

## Nature

The Northern Water Tribe Court is the political, legal, military, religious, and ceremonial leadership centered in the Northern capital. It combines hereditary authority, noble families, master benders, healers, military officers, administrators, and spiritual custodians.

## Composition and work

The court manages defense, canals, food reserves, healing institutions, bending education, marriage and inheritance law, foreign diplomacy, trade, sacred sites, and relations among powerful families.

## Structure

A chief or ruling household stands at the center, supported by advisers, nobles, military commanders, senior healers, waterbending masters, judges, and administrative officials. Tradition carries great weight, but internal reform debates intensified after the war.

## Joining

Court office may be inherited, appointed, earned through military or healing service, or gained through scholarship and administration. Outsiders can become advisers or envoys but rarely receive permanent authority without residence, sponsorship, and political trust.

Waterbending students enter formal schools through family placement, aptitude, sponsorship, or public service programs. Combat and healing tracks may still reflect contested gender traditions, depending on reform progress.

## Character affiliations

- **Pakku:** senior waterbending master, cultural authority, and reform-capable traditionalist.
- **Gran Gran:** originally from the North but no longer subject to its social expectations; respected connection rather than court member.
- **Katara:** internationally respected master and healer whose success challenges restrictive traditions.
- **Aang:** Avatar and diplomatic partner, not a court officer.
- **Hakoda and Sokka:** Southern counterparts in negotiation, not Northern subordinates.
- **Yue:** deceased princess whose sacrifice remains spiritually and politically important.

## Internal blocs

Noble traditionalists, military defenders, healing institutions, gender reformers, spiritual conservatives, merchants seeking southern trade, and officials concerned about loss of authority.

## Membership consequences

Court members gain education, resources, status, and access but face obligations of ceremony, loyalty, family reputation, and public service. Abuse of office, sacrilege, treason, or misuse of public stores can cause dismissal, exile, trial, or loss of title.

## Player interaction

The player may enter as student, healer, guard, scholar, merchant representative, envoy, or appointed specialist. Advancement requires sponsorship, service, and navigation of court politics. One endorsement does not open every institution.

## AI rules

- The North is not culturally uniform.
- Pakku is influential but not sole ruler.
- Spiritual authority, noble status, military rank, and bending mastery are distinct.
- Reform should produce resistance and negotiation.
- Yue's memory must not be used as an automatic command from beyond death.
