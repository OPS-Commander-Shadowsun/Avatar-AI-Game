# Fire Nation Royal Government

## Nature

The Fire Nation Royal Government is the civil state ruled by Fire Lord Zuko. It includes the royal household, ministries, provincial administrators, courts, tax offices, diplomatic staff, palace security, and advisory councils inherited from a century of imperial rule.

## Composition and work

Civil servants manage law, taxation, reconstruction, trade, infrastructure, education, diplomacy, public records, veterans, and provincial administration. Palace officials manage petitions, ceremonies, security, correspondence, and access to the Fire Lord.

## Structure

- Fire Lord;
- royal cabinet and senior ministers;
- ministry directors;
- provincial governors and magistrates;
- civil servants, inspectors, clerks, and local officials;
- palace household and security offices.

Zuko has final royal authority under the default system, but his government is politically constrained by law, bureaucracy, military influence, noble families, provincial interests, public legitimacy, and international agreements.

## Joining

Civil service entry may occur through education, examinations, apprenticeship, provincial appointment, noble sponsorship, or specialist recruitment. Palace access requires stricter background checks. Senior offices require experience, political trust, and public legitimacy.

Foreigners can serve as advisers or diplomats but do not automatically receive internal authority. A player may become a contractor, envoy, investigator, clerk, guard, specialist, or appointed official through sustained service.

## Character affiliations

- **Zuko:** Fire Lord and head of state.
- **Mai:** close royal associate and possible security or intelligence adviser; not automatically a state subordinate.
- **Iroh:** informal adviser with no default office.
- **Azula:** former princess and former appointed Fire Lord; current legal status is a major political issue.
- **Ozai:** deposed and imprisoned former ruler; excluded from lawful authority.
- **Aang:** international partner and mediator, not a Fire Nation official.
- **Sokka and Katara:** foreign advisers or counterparts when invited.

## Internal blocs

Reformers, cautious administrators, imperial loyalists, provincial autonomists, noble conservatives, veterans' advocates, industrial interests, and younger officials seeking merit-based advancement.

## Membership consequences

Officials gain legal access, salary, records, and influence but accept duties, conflicts-of-interest rules, scrutiny, and possible reassignment. Corruption, treason, abuse of office, leaking protected records, or violent loyalism can lead to dismissal, trial, or imprisonment.

## AI rules

- Government orders require a chain of authority and communication time.
- Zuko cannot personally manage every office.
- Officials may be competent, corrupt, afraid, loyal, or conflicted.
- Reform is gradual because institutions and incentives survive changes of ruler.
- Membership does not grant unrestricted access to Zuko or classified archives.
