# Southern Water Tribe Council

## Nature

The Southern Water Tribe Council is the evolving civic leadership of the recovering Southern Water Tribe. It combines traditional family authority, respected elders, returning warriors, healers, builders, hunters, traders, and younger reformers.

## Composition and work

The council coordinates food stores, housing, fishing grounds, defense, medical care, rebuilding, education, trade, dispute resolution, visiting delegations, and the return of displaced families. Its authority depends heavily on community trust rather than distant bureaucracy.

## Structure

Hakoda is the default chief and leading public figure, but he is not an unchecked monarch. Elders, family representatives, specialist committees, and settlement leaders influence decisions. Major war, migration, marriage, land, and trade questions may require broad consultation.

## Joining

Ordinary tribe members participate through family, settlement, craft, or service roles. Formal council membership normally requires adulthood, community standing, demonstrated service, and selection by local custom. Outsiders may become advisers or residents but must earn trust before receiving authority.

Typical paths include:

- community service and residence;
- returning-warrior representation;
- healer, hunter, builder, trader, or teacher appointment;
- election or selection by a settlement;
- sponsorship by elders and public confirmation.

## Character affiliations

- **Hakoda:** chief, wartime commander, and senior council leader.
- **Gran Gran:** elder and cultural authority.
- **Katara:** healer, teacher, advocate, and influential member without automatic supremacy.
- **Sokka:** strategist, inventor, envoy, and policy adviser.
- **Pakku:** Northern-born master connected through family and teaching; adviser rather than default ruler.
- **Aang:** trusted ally and Katara's partner, not a council member by title.

## Internal blocs

Traditional subsistence leaders, reconstruction advocates, military veterans, trade expansionists, families wary of foreign influence, supporters of closer Northern ties, and younger members demanding equal training and broader opportunity.

## Leaving and discipline

Council members may resign, lose community confidence, be recalled by their settlement, or be removed for theft, coercion, collaboration with raiders, or abuse of ration and land authority.

## Player interaction

The player can become a resident, worker, defender, adviser, or council representative through long-term participation. Heroic service earns influence but does not erase kinship, residence, and accountability requirements.

## AI rules

- The Southern Tribe is rebuilding and cannot fund unlimited projects.
- Hakoda, Katara, Sokka, and Gran Gran may disagree.
- Community consent matters more than one outsider's fame.
- Decisions should consider weather, food, transport, labor, and cultural survival.
- Northern customs are not automatically Southern law.
