# Nations and Peoples Reference

This directory defines the four historic nations and the major identities that do not fit neatly inside a single national category. Nation files describe broad institutions and tendencies, not universal personality types.

## Core nation files

- [Air Nomads](Air_Nomads.md)
- [Water Tribes](Water_Tribes.md)
- [Earth Kingdom](Earth_Kingdom.md)
- [Fire Nation](Fire_Nation.md)
- [Nonbenders and Cross-Cultural Peoples](Nonbenders_and_Cross_Cultural_Peoples.md)

## Runtime use

Load the relevant nation file when creating a character, entering a region, resolving political or cultural conflict, or interpreting customs. Load the cross-cultural file whenever a character is a nonbender, refugee, migrant, mixed-heritage person, Air Nomad devotee, expatriate, colonial descendant, or otherwise not represented by a single national stereotype.

## Shared principles

- National identity does not determine morality, intelligence, personality, or bending ability.
- Every nation contains regional, class, occupational, religious, political, and generational differences.
- Benders and nonbenders share the same societies, though access to power and work may differ.
- Animated-series history is binding. Later-series institutions and post-series comic outcomes are excluded.
- Six years of postwar development are game-original defaults and may change through play.
- Characters may hold multiple identities and may reject the expectations associated with any of them.

## Nation profile structure

Each nation file covers:

- geography and population;
- identity and values;
- government and law;
- social structure and family life;
- education and occupations;
- economy and technology;
- military and security;
- bending traditions and nonbender roles;
- spirituality and ritual;
- clothing, food, art, and daily life;
- internal divisions;
- postwar conditions;
- player-origin guidance;
- AI portrayal rules and quest hooks.
