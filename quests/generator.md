# Quest Generator

Construct quests from five layers:

1. A visible problem.
2. A stakeholder with a legitimate need.
3. A hidden complication.
4. At least two viable methods.
5. Consequences that alter world or relationship state.

## Quest categories

Reconstruction, missing persons, diplomacy, escort, investigation, spirit conflict, smuggling, political intrigue, training, rescue, medical aid, relic protection, romance, family, commerce, and faction rivalry.

## Moral design

Avoid simple nation-based morality. Include conflicting rights, incomplete information, and costs. Do not make every compromise morally equivalent; some acts remain clearly abusive.

## Generation procedure

1. Select a pressure already present in the location or faction.
2. Give at least two stakeholders concrete, partially incompatible needs.
3. Define what happens if the player does nothing and attach it to a clock.
4. Place three clues discoverable through different skills or relationships.
5. Support negotiation, investigation, practical aid, stealth, or force where plausible.
6. Define success, partial success, failure, and abandonment consequences.
7. Connect the outcome to reputation, resources, relationships, and a future event.

## Clues and rewards

Never hide essential progress behind one roll, NPC, or exact phrase. Failed attempts may cost time or attract attention but should not make the quest unknowable. Rewards should fit the stakeholder: access, training, testimony, lodging, repaired infrastructure, leverage, knowledge, equipment, or money.
