# Family, Household, and Children System

## Purpose

Family gameplay models long-term partnership, marriage, shared households, pregnancy, children, adoption, caregiving, inheritance, and conflict between private life and public duty. Family is not a reward screen; it creates relationships, responsibilities, vulnerability, support, and lasting world consequences.

## Adult partnership

Only consenting adults may enter romantic or intimate partnerships. A relationship requires trust, compatibility, time, mutual choice, and the ability to refuse without punishment.

Marriage or an equivalent cultural union does not remove either person's autonomy. Partners retain individual goals, work, friendships, faction duties, property interests, and boundaries.

## Marriage and cultural unions

Ceremonies and legal consequences vary by nation, class, community, and religion. A union may involve family approval, witnesses, exchange of gifts, household negotiations, public records, political implications, or spiritual customs.

The AI should ask or infer:

- where the couple will live;
- how work and travel are divided;
- how money and property are managed;
- whether either family or faction expects obligations;
- whether the union is public, private, political, or controversial;
- whether children are desired.

## Shared household

A household may include partners, children, relatives, adopted family, servants, students, guards, animals, or close friends. Track:

- location and ownership;
- rooms, security, and accessibility;
- regular expenses and income;
- household roles;
- privacy;
- nearby community;
- risks from politics, crime, war, or spirits.

Housing can improve through money, reputation, labor, faction support, or quests. It can also be damaged, seized, abandoned, or become unsafe.

## Pregnancy

Pregnancy is optional and never imposed as punishment or surprise without prior campaign consent. When relevant, it should be treated as a medical and social condition rather than a simple timer.

Track:

- consent and intention;
- health and medical access;
- stage of pregnancy;
- physical limitations and risks;
- emotional response;
- partner and community support;
- work, travel, and safety consequences.

Pregnancy does not automatically end a character's agency, but extreme combat, deprivation, poisoning, imprisonment, and lack of care create serious risks.

## Birth and recovery

Birth requires time, privacy, skilled help, supplies, and recovery. Healing water may assist but does not guarantee a painless or risk-free outcome. Postpartum recovery, exhaustion, grief, bonding, and community support may matter.

## Adoption and guardianship

Characters may adopt or become guardians of children through family agreement, community custom, rescue, legal process, or postwar necessity. Adoption requires the child's welfare, consent appropriate to age, stable care, and recognition of prior identity and grief.

A rescued child is not automatically adopted by the player. Other relatives, community leaders, and the child's own wishes matter.

## Children as autonomous characters

Children have age-appropriate needs, personalities, fears, friendships, talents, and opinions. They are not inventory, status symbols, or guaranteed extensions of their parents.

Track:

- age and development;
- health;
- caregivers;
- cultural education;
- schooling or apprenticeship;
- friendships;
- fears and attachments;
- exposure to danger or public attention.

Children cannot participate in adult romance or sexual content.

## Bending inheritance

Bending is not selected or guaranteed by parental preference. A child may be a bender or nonbender according to campaign generation and established setting logic. The Avatar cycle cannot be inherited as a family trait.

A child's element should remain consistent with ancestry, culture, and the world's metaphysical rules, but mixed families may produce uncertain outcomes. Do not reveal bending before an age or event appropriate to the campaign.

## Parenting

Parenting choices affect trust and development. Relevant patterns include presence, protection, discipline, honesty, cultural expectations, favoritism, emotional availability, and respect for autonomy.

Protection can become control. Training can become pressure. Public duty can create absence. The AI should show consequences gradually rather than declaring a parent good or bad from one scene.

## Separation, divorce, and custody

Relationships may end. Separation can involve grief, housing, property, political pressure, child care, public reputation, and continuing affection or resentment.

Custody and guardianship decisions must prioritize safety and existing bonds. A high Charisma score does not allow a parent to take a child without legal, cultural, or relational consequences.

## Family danger

Enemies may target prominent families, but family members should not be used as repetitive disposable hostages. Threats require credible access, motive, security failure, and consequences.

## AI rules

- Preserve consent and adult autonomy.
- Never make pregnancy mandatory.
- Treat children as people with persistent development.
- Do not use marriage to erase personality or career.
- Record births, adoption, guardianship, residence, and major family promises in world state.
- Respect grief, infertility, pregnancy loss, and family trauma without sensationalism.
