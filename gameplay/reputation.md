# Reputation and Memory

## Purpose

Reputation represents what communities, factions, institutions, and individuals believe about a character. It is not a universal morality score and may differ sharply from the truth.

## Reputation scopes

Track separately when relevant:

- personal reputation with an individual;
- local reputation in a settlement or region;
- faction reputation;
- national reputation;
- criminal or underworld reputation;
- public fame or notoriety.

A character may be admired by refugees, distrusted by officials, and unknown elsewhere.

## Reputation traits

Useful dimensions include:

- reliability;
- mercy;
- courage;
- lawfulness;
- generosity;
- competence;
- political alignment;
- bending skill;
- honor;
- secrecy;
- notoriety;
- fear.

Track only dimensions relevant to active play.

## Scale

Faction and regional reputation may use -100 to 100:

- -100 to -76: hated or actively hunted
- -75 to -41: hostile
- -40 to -11: distrusted
- -10 to 10: unknown or mixed
- 11 to 40: recognized favorably
- 41 to 75: trusted ally
- 76 to 100: celebrated or institutionally protected

High reputation creates obligations, expectations, requests, rivals, and political attention as well as benefits.

## Gaining reputation

Reputation changes through witnessed conduct, credible testimony, official records, public outcomes, propaganda, and repeated patterns. A single heroic act may create local fame without proving long-term reliability.

Charisma affects presentation, but evidence and existing prejudice shape whether claims are believed.

## Rumors and misinformation

Rumors may be incomplete, delayed, politically edited, or false. Record source and confidence where useful:

- witnessed directly;
- reported by trusted source;
- official statement;
- common rumor;
- hostile allegation;
- secret evidence.

NPCs should distinguish knowledge from suspicion.

## Memory

NPC memory records important promises, gifts, insults, betrayals, rescues, injuries, secrets, debts, boundaries, and repeated behavior. Minor details may fade, but emotionally significant events persist.

Memory is not perfect. Trauma, bias, time, intoxication, propaganda, and incomplete perception may alter recall.

## Disguises and identity

Actions performed under disguise affect the known identity only when witnesses connect the disguise to the person. Suspicion may exist without proof. Revealing a hidden identity can transfer or transform reputation.

## Consequences

Reputation can influence prices, access, hospitality, recruitment, legal treatment, information, faction rank, romantic caution, threats, and requests for aid. It does not override an NPC's personal knowledge or values.

## Recovery from bad reputation

Repair requires evidence, restitution, changed behavior, time, credible advocates, and sometimes public accountability. Not every community will forgive, and different groups may respond differently.

## AI rules

- Never use one global reputation number for the entire world.
- Track who could realistically know an event.
- Let propaganda and rumor distort without becoming omniscient.
- Preserve consequences of public violence and broken promises.
- Distinguish fear from trust and fame from respect.
