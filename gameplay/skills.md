# Special Skill System

## Purpose

Special skills describe trained disciplines narrower than the five core attributes. They distinguish two characters with similar attributes and provide clear prerequisites for advanced actions.

## Scale

Skills use ratings from 0 to 5.

- **0 — Untrained:** no reliable competence.
- **1 — Familiar:** basic instruction or limited experience.
- **2 — Practiced:** useful under ordinary conditions.
- **3 — Professional:** dependable field competence.
- **4 — Expert:** advanced specialist capable of teaching fundamentals.
- **5 — Mastered:** rare mastery demonstrated across difficult conditions.

## Starting allocation

At character creation, choose:

- one primary skill at 3;
- two supporting skills at 2;
- two familiar skills at 1.

All other skills begin at 0. This provides 9 total starting skill points, but the distribution is fixed to prevent a character from beginning as a master.

A broad combat skill does not grant mastery of every weapon. Characters should record specific forms or weapons when relevant.

## Bending-related skills

Bending power describes raw elemental capability and overall command. Subskills describe rare or specialized techniques. Examples include:

- Healing
- Bloodbending
- Plantbending
- Seismic Sense
- Metalbending
- Sandbending
- Lavabending
- Lightning Generation
- Lightning Redirection
- Combustion Technique
- Advanced Glider Arts
- Energybending

These skills cannot be selected merely because points are available. They require compatible bending, aptitude, instruction, physical conditions, and narrative access. Energybending is not a normal player progression skill.

## Attribute and skill interaction

An action normally combines one core attribute with one relevant skill.

Examples:

- Intelligence + Strategy for a battle plan.
- Dexterity + Throwing Weapons for a precise disabling strike.
- Charisma + Diplomacy for negotiation.
- Intelligence + Medicine for diagnosis.
- Stealth + Investigation for covert surveillance.
- Dexterity + Bending Power for rapid technical forms.

The AI should select the combination that best matches the method, not simply the player's highest values.

## Learning and improvement

Skills improve through use, instruction, reflection, access to tools, and meaningful downtime. Repetition without challenge does not guarantee advancement.

Suggested requirements:

- 0 to 1: introductory teaching and practice.
- 1 to 2: repeated safe application.
- 2 to 3: field experience and demonstrated reliability.
- 3 to 4: expert instruction, difficult projects, or major missions.
- 4 to 5: years of focused mastery or a defining character arc.

Failure can contribute to learning when the character understands the cause and survives the consequences.

## Teachers and access

A willing teacher may require trust, payment, service, faction membership, cultural respect, or proof of responsibility. Named masters retain autonomy and can refuse dangerous students.

Books and observation may teach theory, but complex physical skills require practice and correction. Secret techniques should not spread instantly after one encounter.

## Skill limits

Skills do not provide impossible knowledge. Medicine cannot identify an unknown poison without evidence. Strategy cannot predict irrational choices perfectly. Stealth cannot erase witnesses. Spirituality cannot force spirits to cooperate.

## Respecialization

Characters may change focus over time, but previously learned skills are not casually erased. Long inactivity may create rust, represented through temporary difficulty rather than automatic permanent point loss.
