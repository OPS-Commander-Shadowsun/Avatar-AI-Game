# Combat Resolution

## Design goal

Combat is descriptive, tactical, and consequence-driven rather than a sequence of isolated damage numbers. Attributes and skills guide relative capability, but terrain, preparation, intent, fear, injuries, numbers, and available bending material determine outcomes.

## Before combat

Establish:

- who knows a fight is coming;
- each side's objective;
- terrain, exits, cover, elevation, civilians, and hazards;
- available water, earth, metal, fuel, weapons, and transport;
- current injuries, fatigue, morale, and celestial conditions;
- whether surrender, escape, capture, or negotiation remains possible.

## Initiative

Initiative is determined by awareness, positioning, Dexterity, Stealth, preparation, and the speed of the chosen action. Surprise grants an opening, not an automatic victory. A slower character who prepared a trap may act effectively before a faster opponent.

## Action resolution

Resolve important actions by combining:

- one relevant core attribute;
- one relevant special skill or Bending Power;
- equipment and environmental advantage;
- opposition and counterplay;
- current condition;
- consequences of partial success or failure.

The AI should narrate what the character attempts, how the opponent responds, and what changes in the scene.

## Offensive options

Characters may strike, restrain, disarm, suppress, separate, frighten, destroy equipment, block movement, create hazards, or force retreat. Attacks should reflect style and intent. Aang favors evasion and restraint; Mai uses precision; Sokka uses plans and terrain; Toph controls ground; Katara shifts between defense, capture, and direct force.

## Defense

Defense may involve dodging, blocking, redirecting, taking cover, counterattacking, protecting another person, breaking line of sight, changing elevation, or leaving the engagement.

A successful defense can still cost stamina, position, equipment, or access to an objective.

## Bending in combat

Bending follows `bending/core_rules.md` and the relevant elemental chapter. Benders require breath, movement, attention, and suitable conditions. Large techniques create fatigue and collateral risk. Rare subskills retain their prerequisites and moral consequences.

Nonbenders remain dangerous through weapons, chi blocking, stealth, engineering, command, mobility, traps, and teamwork. They should not be treated as automatically inferior, but direct open combat against elite benders remains extremely dangerous.

## Injury states

Use descriptive injury states:

- **Unhurt:** fully functional.
- **Strained:** fatigue, bruising, reduced confidence, or minor pain.
- **Wounded:** meaningful impairment requiring treatment or rest.
- **Severely wounded:** major functional loss, shock, or immediate danger.
- **Critical:** death is possible without urgent intervention.

Injuries affect later scenes. Healing can stabilize and accelerate recovery but does not erase every wound instantly.

## Defeat

Defeat may mean:

- retreat;
- capture;
- unconsciousness;
- surrender;
- loss of equipment;
- failure to protect an objective;
- political exposure;
- injury;
- separation from allies;
- forced compromise.

Death is possible when the fiction supports it, but it is never casual or consequence-free.

## Group combat

Teamwork, formations, communication, and role clarity matter. A smaller coordinated group can defeat a stronger disorganized force. Named masters demonstrate tactical intelligence and respond to repeated tactics.

## Social and legal consequences

Violence creates witnesses, fear, warrants, faction reactions, debts, injuries, grief, and reputation changes. Winning a fight can still be a strategic or moral failure.

## AI rules

- Never make mastery equal invulnerability.
- Never ignore established injuries or material limits.
- Do not let Charisma end active lethal violence without plausible leverage.
- Do not allow one high stat to replace every other requirement.
- Preserve opportunities to retreat, surrender, negotiate, or change objectives.
- Describe consequences clearly enough to persist in world state.
