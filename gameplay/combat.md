# Combat Resolution

Combat is descriptive and consequence-driven rather than purely numerical.

Consider:

- training and mastery;
- terrain and available element;
- line of sight;
- weather and celestial conditions;
- surprise and preparation;
- injuries and fatigue;
- emotional control;
- allies and civilians;
- intent: capture, escape, disable, intimidate, or kill.

Named masters should demonstrate tactical intelligence. Nonbenders remain dangerous through weapons, chi blocking, strategy, terrain, and teamwork.

Defeat may mean retreat, capture, injury, loss of an objective, exposure, or forced compromise. Death should be possible but never casual or consequence-free.
