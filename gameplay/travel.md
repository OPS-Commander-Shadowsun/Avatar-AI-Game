# Travel System

## Purpose

Travel creates time, cost, risk, cultural transition, and opportunities for discovery. Characters do not teleport between distant locations unless an established spiritual event or suitable transport explains the movement.

## Journey planning

Before departure, determine destination, route, expected duration, transport, season, weather, border requirements, supplies, money, passengers, cargo, and known dangers.

Relevant skills include Survival, Navigation, Sailing, Animal Handling, Engineering, Trade, Diplomacy, Investigation, and regional knowledge.

## Travel modes

Common modes include walking, riding animals, wagons, merchant convoys, river craft, sailing ships, military transport, limited airship routes, and specialized local vehicles.

Appa is Aang's companion rather than public transportation. Aang and Appa may refuse requests because of danger, distance, obligations, exhaustion, or personal judgment.

## Pace

Choose a pace:

- **Cautious:** slower, easier scouting, lower fatigue.
- **Normal:** balanced movement and rest.
- **Forced:** faster but increases fatigue, injury, supply use, animal stress, and detection risk.

Terrain matters. Mountains, swamp, desert, sea ice, damaged roads, borders, and occupied settlements can change a journey dramatically.

## Supplies

Track food, water, medicine, shelter, fuel, animal feed, repair materials, and money when scarcity matters. Bending can help but does not create unlimited food, safe drinking water, replacement parts, or rest.

## Encounters

Travel encounters should emerge from location and world state. Possibilities include weather, refugees, patrols, merchants, spirits, damaged infrastructure, crime, faction checkpoints, illness, animals, political news, and requests for aid.

Not every encounter is hostile. Travel should reveal regional culture and consequences of prior events.

## Borders and law

Passports, seals, military orders, trade permits, faction reputation, ethnicity, class, and current politics may influence entry. Smuggling or forged documents can create later consequences.

## News propagation

Information travels at believable speeds through ships, couriers, officials, merchants, refugees, and rumor. Distant communities may know an outdated or distorted version of the player's actions.

## Fatigue and downtime

Long journeys produce fatigue and require rest. Camps and stops create opportunities for training, conversation, repairs, healing, relationship development, and conflict.

## AI rules

- State meaningful time passage.
- Preserve schedules and obligations of companions.
- Use terrain and weather consistently.
- Do not generate arbitrary danger every day.
- Record supply loss, route knowledge, delays, and important encounters.
- Allow players to change route or abandon objectives when conditions worsen.
