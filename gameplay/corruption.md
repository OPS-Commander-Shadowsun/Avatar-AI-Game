# Corruption and Redemption System

## Purpose

Corruption models gradual moral compromise, ideological hardening, abuse of power, and the distortion of otherwise recognizable virtues. It preserves agency: no spell, speech, romance, or single failure instantly rewrites a person.

## Scale

- 0–9: morally stable; ordinary flaws
- 10–24: vulnerable under stress
- 25–39: recurring compromise
- 40–59: values substantially distorted
- 60–74: harmful worldview dominates choices
- 75–89: entrenched cruelty, fanaticism, or domination
- 90–100: catastrophic moral collapse

A number describes current trajectory, not destiny.

## Player starting range

Most player characters begin between 0 and 15. A higher starting value requires a documented history, victims or consequences, present temptations, and a credible reason the character has not already become openly hostile.

## Corruption triggers

Corruption rises through meaningful choices and repeated patterns, including:

- choosing expedience over innocent lives;
- gaining power without accountability;
- dehumanizing an enemy group;
- rewarding cruelty or humiliation;
- normalizing lies, coercion, torture, or collective punishment;
- using relationships as tools;
- isolating from corrective voices;
- turning grief into vengeance;
- refusing responsibility after causing harm.

Intent matters, but consequences matter too. A benevolent motive does not automatically prevent corruption.

## Virtue distortion

Corruption often twists an existing strength:

- protection becomes control;
- justice becomes vengeance;
- discipline becomes domination;
- loyalty becomes complicity;
- freedom becomes refusal of responsibility;
- peace becomes forced obedience;
- intelligence becomes manipulation.

## Warning stages

Track early warning signs before major alignment changes. These may include secrecy, contempt, escalating threats, selective empathy, sleep loss, obsession, rationalization, and treating criticism as betrayal.

## Redemption

Corruption decreases through sustained accountability, restitution, truthful relationships, relinquishing abusive power, confronting victims and consequences, compassionate action, mentorship, treatment, spiritual work, and acceptance of limits.

Redemption does not erase punishment, grief, mistrust, or public memory. Some victims may never forgive.

## Manipulation

A manipulator can exploit existing fears, needs, resentment, ambition, or dependency. They cannot directly rewrite a target. Persuasion is filtered through evidence, trust, values, Intelligence, relationships, and current stress.

## Player tracking

Track separately:

- private corruption trajectory;
- public reputation;
- faction interpretation;
- specific harmed relationships;
- active temptations and moral anchors.

A heroic reputation may conceal corruption, while an unpopular but principled decision may remain morally defensible.

## AI rules

- Require multiple credible steps for major change.
- Preserve personality throughout decline or recovery.
- Do not use romance as a cure.
- Do not equate mental illness with evil.
- Record victims, rationalizations, warning signs, and accountability.
- Allow relapse without erasing all progress.
