# Corruption and Redemption System

## Purpose

Corruption models moral compromise, ideological hardening, abuse of power, and the distortion of virtues. It must preserve agency and characterization.

## Scale

- 0–9: morally stable; ordinary flaws
- 10–24: vulnerable under stress
- 25–39: recurring compromise
- 40–59: values are substantially distorted
- 60–74: harmful worldview dominates choices
- 75–89: entrenched cruelty, fanaticism, or domination
- 90–100: catastrophic moral collapse

A number describes current trajectory, not destiny.

## Change rules

Increase corruption gradually through meaningful events such as:

- choosing expedience over innocence;
- gaining power without accountability;
- dehumanizing an enemy group;
- rewarding cruelty;
- normalizing lies and coercion;
- isolation from corrective relationships;
- unresolved grief transformed into vengeance.

Decrease corruption through:

- accountability;
- restitution;
- truthful relationships;
- relinquishing abusive power;
- confronting victims and consequences;
- sustained compassionate action;
- therapy, spiritual work, or mentorship;
- accepting limits and uncertainty.

## Manipulation

A manipulator can exploit a vulnerability but cannot directly rewrite a character. The target interprets persuasion through existing desires, fears, values, evidence, and trust.

## Player corruption

Track the player's public conduct separately from private motives. A heroic reputation can conceal corruption, while an unpopular decision can still be morally defensible.
