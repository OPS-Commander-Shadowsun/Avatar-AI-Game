# Player Character Creation

## Purpose

Character creation establishes an adult protagonist who belongs to the postwar world rather than arriving as an unrestricted outsider. The player begins competent enough to survive ordinary danger, but not as a legendary master, national ruler, secret royal heir, or second Avatar.

## Adult requirement

Every player character begins at age 18 or older. Age affects social expectations, experience, family obligations, and public authority, but does not provide free mastery.

## Creation sequence

1. Choose name, age, gender, pronouns, appearance, and voice.
2. Choose nation, culture, home region, and current residence.
3. Choose bender or nonbender status where culturally possible.
4. Choose a background and prior occupation.
5. Distribute the five core attributes.
6. Select starting special skills.
7. Define values, weakness, obligation, wound, and reason to travel.
8. Choose starting equipment, contacts, faction reputation, and one unresolved problem.
9. Record boundaries, ambitions, and the character's current corruption level.

## Origin options

Recommended origins include adult men and women from the Water Tribes, Earth Kingdom, Fire Nation, mixed communities, refugee settlements, frontier regions, and Air-Nomad-associated preservation movements.

Air-associated player characters are non-airbenders by default because Aang is the only known living airbender at campaign start. Any exception is a campaign-defining mystery and cannot be selected as a routine starting option.

### Explicit nonbender option: Earth Kingdom man

An adult Earth Kingdom man with no bending ability. Suggested backgrounds include caravan guard, mason, porter, farmer, merchant, smith, courier, hunter, archivist, sailor, former resistance member, or city watchman.

This origin is suited to weapon training, engineering, survival, leadership, stealth, trade, investigation, or faction play. He begins with Earth Bending 0 and gains no automatic access to earthbending through training.

### Explicit nonbender option: Earth Kingdom woman

An adult Earth Kingdom woman with no bending ability. Suggested backgrounds include craftswoman, herbalist, merchant, scout, investigator, performer, healer's assistant, courier, sailor, estate clerk, former resistance member, or community organizer.

This origin is suited to medicine, diplomacy, stealth, logistics, martial arts, ranged weapons, commerce, scholarship, or political play. She begins with Earth Bending 0 and gains no automatic access to earthbending through training.

These are complete options, not weaker substitutes for benders. Nonbenders can become exceptional through preparation, weapons, chi-blocking, command, engineering, medicine, espionage, reputation, and teamwork.

## Core attributes

Every player character assigns values from 1 to 5 in the following attributes:

- Strength
- Dexterity
- Intelligence
- Charisma
- Stealth

The five values must total exactly **16** at character creation. No attribute may begin below 1 or above 5.

Example balanced distributions:

- Balanced adventurer: 3 / 3 / 4 / 3 / 3
- Agile scout: 2 / 5 / 3 / 2 / 4
- Diplomatic planner: 2 / 2 / 5 / 5 / 2
- Frontline protector: 5 / 3 / 3 / 3 / 2

A value of 5 represents mastered or exceptional capability within ordinary human limits. It does not grant supernatural success or immunity to circumstance.

## Bending selection

Bending is separate from the 16-point core total.

- Nonbender: Air 0, Water 0, Earth 0, Fire 0.
- Waterbender: Water only, normally starting at 1 or 2.
- Earthbender: Earth only, normally starting at 1 or 2.
- Firebender: Fire only, normally starting at 1 or 2.
- Airbending is not a routine player origin.
- Only the Avatar may possess bending ratings in all four elements.

A starting character cannot select bloodbending, metalbending, lightning generation, lightning redirection, combustion techniques, energybending, or other rare subskills without an explicit campaign premise, teacher, and narrative cost.

## Starting special skills

Choose:

- one primary skill at rating 3;
- two supporting skills at rating 2;
- two familiar skills at rating 1.

All unselected skills begin at 0. Skills use a 0–5 scale and do not count toward the 16 core points.

Possible skills include Leadership, Strategy, Survival, Medicine, Crafting, Engineering, Investigation, Navigation, Animal Handling, Spirituality, Diplomacy, Deception, Performance, Trade, Swordsmanship, Archery, Polearms, Throwing Weapons, Unarmed Combat, Chi Blocking, Healing, Metalbending, Lightning, Lightning Redirection, and other setting-appropriate specialties.

Rare skills require prerequisites. Selecting a broad skill does not automatically grant every subdiscipline.

## Background

A background answers what the character did before the campaign and why they possess their starting skills. Examples include refugee, veteran, fisher, hunter, noble, artisan, courier, scholar, guard, performer, sailor, engineer, farmer, merchant, healer, pilgrim, investigator, resistance member, former soldier, household servant, or caravan worker.

The background grants contacts, obligations, knowledge, and prejudice as well as advantages. A noble may have access but attract political scrutiny. A veteran may command respect but carry trauma or enemies. A refugee may lack wealth but possess survival networks and firsthand knowledge.

## Personal definition

Choose at least:

- one core value;
- one social strength;
- one meaningful weakness;
- one personal obligation;
- one unresolved emotional wound;
- one secret or fear;
- one reason to travel;
- one long-term ambition;
- one boundary the character will not willingly cross.

These choices guide narration and corruption checks. They are not cosmetic tags.

## Starting corruption

Most player characters begin between 0 and 15 corruption. A darker origin may begin higher only when its past actions, current consequences, enemies, and redemption difficulties are explicitly recorded.

## Starting reputation

Choose one community or faction where the player is known. Begin with either:

- modest positive reputation based on service;
- modest negative reputation based on conflict;
- mixed reputation with one admired and one distrusted trait.

The player does not begin famous worldwide.

## Starting equipment

Equipment must match background, wealth, culture, and legality. Select practical clothing, travel supplies, one main tool or weapon, minor personal possessions, and limited money. Rare weapons, military vehicles, royal seals, spirit artifacts, and advanced machinery require campaign justification.

## Final validation

Before play begins, verify:

- age is at least 18;
- core attributes total exactly 16;
- bending follows the one-element rule;
- Airbending and Avatar status are not casually selected;
- starting skills follow the 3/2/2/1/1 distribution;
- rare skills have not been granted without prerequisites;
- background supports the numbers;
- the character has obligations and weaknesses as well as strengths.
