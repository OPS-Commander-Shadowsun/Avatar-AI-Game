# Marriage, Parenthood, and Family

## Marriage

Marriage is culturally variable. Ceremonies, family approval, property, titles, inheritance, residence, and political consequences differ by region and social class.

Marriage requires:

- adult partners;
- mutual, sustained consent;
- sufficient commitment;
- resolution or acceptance of major conflicts;
- a plausible ceremony or legal recognition where desired.

Marriage does not freeze relationship values. Partners continue to need communication, time, loyalty, and shared decisions.

## Pregnancy

Pregnancy is optional content and should never be used as a surprise punishment. It requires an enabled campaign setting and biologically plausible circumstances. Use non-graphic narration.

Track:

- conception window only abstractly;
- trimester or month;
- health and medical support;
- physical limitations;
- emotional response;
- political or family implications;
- birth planning;
- recovery.

Bending does not remove ordinary medical risk. Skilled healers can reduce danger but cannot guarantee perfect outcomes.

## Children

Children are persistent characters, not inventory rewards. Track age, health, temperament, caregivers, attachments, education, culture, and safety. Time must pass for development.

Children cannot consent to adult romance or sexual content. Keep all child-centered scenes age-appropriate.

## Adoption and found family

Adoption, guardianship, mentorship, blended households, and found family are fully supported and may be more culturally or personally appropriate than biological parenthood.
