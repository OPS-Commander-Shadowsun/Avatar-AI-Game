# Core Attribute System

## Purpose

Core attributes describe broad capability shared by player characters, major NPCs, and secondary NPCs. They guide relative difficulty and narration but never replace context, preparation, injury, equipment, relationships, or consequences.

## Scale

Each attribute ranges from 1 to 5.

- **1 — Unskilled:** limited training or serious weakness.
- **2 — Basic:** functional but below professional level.
- **3 — Skilled:** reliable adult competence.
- **4 — Expert:** exceptional professional ability.
- **5 — Mastered:** among the best practitioners likely to appear in ordinary play.

A rating of 5 is not omnipotence. Masters can fail against superior preparation, bad terrain, injury, numbers, or unfamiliar problems.

## Point total

Every standard player character begins with exactly **16 total points** across the five core attributes. Each attribute must remain between 1 and 5.

`Strength + Dexterity + Intelligence + Charisma + Stealth = 16`

Bending power and special skills are separate and do not count toward this total.

## Strength

Strength measures physical force, lifting, grappling, striking power, carrying capacity, and resistance to forced movement. It contributes to endurance but does not replace conditioning or health.

Strength is useful for climbing, breaking obstacles, carrying injured people, maintaining heavy weapons, resisting restraints, and close combat.

## Dexterity

Dexterity measures balance, coordination, speed, precision, reflexes, fine motor control, and efficient movement. It supports acrobatics, weapon accuracy, many bending forms, dodging, climbing, and delicate work.

Dexterity does not automatically grant stealth or tactical judgment.

## Intelligence

Intelligence measures analysis, memory, planning, technical understanding, pattern recognition, scholarship, and learning speed. It supports strategy, engineering, medicine, investigation, languages, and understanding unfamiliar systems.

High Intelligence does not grant secret information, perfect judgment, or emotional wisdom.

## Charisma

Charisma measures presence, communication, empathy, leadership, persuasion, performance, intimidation, and the ability to shape group attention.

Charisma does not override consent, loyalty, ideology, evidence, or established relationships. It creates opportunities; it does not mind-control NPCs.

## Stealth

Stealth measures concealment, silent movement, disguise habits, observation avoidance, ambush preparation, and blending into social or physical environments.

Stealth depends heavily on terrain, clothing, witnesses, lighting, sound, cultural familiarity, and prior suspicion.

## Attribute checks

When uncertain outcomes matter, the AI should consider:

- relevant attribute;
- applicable special skill;
- equipment and preparation;
- opposition capability;
- environmental advantage;
- injury, fatigue, fear, and time pressure;
- cost of failure.

Do not resolve every ordinary action with a check. Use checks when failure would change the situation.

## Growth

Core attributes improve slowly. Raising an attribute requires sustained training, changed lifestyle, or a major developmental arc. Values above 5 are not used for ordinary characters.

Temporary bonuses should be represented through favorable circumstances, equipment, allies, or scene-specific advantage rather than permanently changing the sheet.
