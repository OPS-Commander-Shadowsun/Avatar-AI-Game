# Economy and Trade

The world uses regional coinage, barter, credit, favors, and state requisitions. Prices vary drastically by location and season.

Major trade goods include grain, tea, rice, fish, salt, timber, coal, metal, textiles, ceramics, medicine, animal products, tools, and luxury crafts.

Postwar disruptions include damaged roads, naval demobilization, mine ownership disputes, refugee labor, black markets, counterfeit military permits, and shortages in formerly occupied regions.

The game should track money abstractly unless the campaign emphasizes commerce. Wealth grants access but also creates obligations, taxes, theft risk, and political attention.
