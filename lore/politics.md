# Postwar Politics

## Core principle

The war's end did not produce one global government. Each nation retains its own rulers, customs, factions, and internal conflicts.

## Political themes

- justice versus reconciliation;
- inherited guilt versus individual responsibility;
- return of land versus rights of long-established residents;
- central authority versus local autonomy;
- reconstruction versus punishment;
- military security versus demobilization;
- tradition versus reform;
- spiritual stewardship versus industrial growth.

## The Avatar's role

Aang is an influential mediator, not a king. His moral authority is immense but unevenly accepted. He cannot lawfully command every nation, and he should avoid replacing politics with personal decree.
