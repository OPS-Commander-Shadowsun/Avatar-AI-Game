# World History Foundation

This document provides the historical framework required by the game master. It summarizes the setting in original language and defines what historical pressures still shape the campaign six years after Fire Lord Ozai's defeat.

## The Four Nations and the Avatar Cycle

Human civilization developed around four broad cultural spheres associated with water, earth, fire, and air. These nations were never internally uniform. Each contained regional customs, political disputes, class divisions, and different attitudes toward bending. Benders shaped warfare, transport, agriculture, architecture, healing, ritual, and daily labor, while nonbenders remained essential as rulers, artisans, soldiers, sailors, scholars, inventors, merchants, and community leaders.

The Avatar is reborn in a repeating cycle through the four nations and can learn all four elements. The Avatar is not an absolute monarch. The office combines spiritual responsibility, mediation, diplomacy, and—when necessary—the use of force. Past Avatars made imperfect decisions, and their legacies may be interpreted differently by different cultures.

## Fire Lord Sozin and Imperial Expansion

Fire Lord Sozin inherited a prosperous, industrializing nation. He came to believe that Fire Nation strength and wealth justified expansion beyond its islands. His ideology mixed genuine pride in national achievement with paternalism, entitlement, and the conviction that resistance represented ignorance rather than self-determination.

Avatar Roku, once Sozin's friend, rejected this expansion. Their relationship deteriorated as Sozin tested the limits of the Avatar's opposition. Roku later died during a volcanic catastrophe after Sozin chose not to save him. This removed the strongest immediate obstacle to the Fire Nation's plans.

## Aang's Disappearance and the Destruction of the Air Nomads

Aang learned that he was the Avatar earlier than tradition required because Air Nomad leaders feared the coming crisis. Overwhelmed by responsibility and threatened with separation from Monk Gyatso, he fled the Southern Air Temple with Appa. A storm forced them into the ocean, where the Avatar State preserved them inside an iceberg.

During Aang's absence, Sozin used the power of a comet to launch coordinated attacks on the Air Temples. The assault destroyed the known Air Nomad population. The genocide was both strategic and ideological: the Fire Nation sought to kill the new Avatar and eliminate a culture that might oppose imperial domination.

The destruction of the Air Nomads remains a living wound. Their temples contain sacred spaces, personal remains, abandoned art, damaged records, and dangerous relics. Treasure hunting, tourism, scholarship, spiritual pilgrimage, and political appropriation all create potential conflict at these sites.

## The Hundred Year War

The Fire Nation spent a century expanding into Earth Kingdom territory, attacking maritime routes, suppressing resistance, and raiding the Water Tribes. The war did not advance evenly. Some regions experienced direct occupation; others endured taxes, conscription, displaced populations, shortages, propaganda, or intermittent violence.

Fire Nation colonies became increasingly complex over generations. Some remained military outposts. Others developed mixed families, local trade networks, and residents born far from the Fire Nation homeland. At the campaign start, these communities cannot be resolved through a simple division between invaders and victims. Land theft, collaboration, inheritance, intermarriage, and local identity overlap.

The Southern Water Tribe suffered repeated raids targeting waterbenders. Its population, defenses, and traditions were badly weakened. The Northern Water Tribe survived behind formidable defenses and retained greater resources, but its isolation preserved restrictive customs and encouraged political caution.

The Earth Kingdom's enormous size prevented complete conquest, but also weakened unified resistance. Provincial rulers, local militias, merchants, bandits, secret societies, and city governments often acted independently. Ba Sing Se's leadership concealed the war from much of its population, allowing the Dai Li to turn stability into authoritarian control.

## Return of the Avatar

Katara and Sokka discovered Aang in the Southern Ocean. Their journey with him gradually formed the group later called Team Avatar. Aang learned waterbending from Katara, earthbending from Toph Beifong, and firebending from Zuko after Zuko rejected his father's regime.

The group encountered communities across the world and became personally involved in resistance, rescue operations, spiritual crises, political conspiracies, and military planning. These experiences matter because canon characters do not know the story as an audience does. Each character knows only what they witnessed, were told, or could reasonably infer.

## Collapse of Ozai's War Government

During the final phase of the war, Ozai intended to use the comet's power to devastate Earth Kingdom lands and destroy organized resistance. Aang defeated him without killing him, using energybending to remove his firebending. Zuko and Katara defeated Azula after her mental stability collapsed under pressure, isolation, fear, and betrayal. Iroh and the Order of the White Lotus liberated Ba Sing Se, while Sokka, Toph, and Suki disabled the Fire Nation airship fleet.

Zuko became Fire Lord and declared that the Fire Nation would help restore balance. His coronation ended Ozai's formal rule, but did not instantly transform the military, noble houses, civil service, schools, factories, or public beliefs shaped by a century of imperialism.

## The Six-Year Bridge

Everything after the animated finale is game-original unless explicitly labeled otherwise.

During the six years before campaign start:

- Zuko has pursued reform while surviving resistance from officers, nobles, industrial interests, and ideological loyalists.
- Aang has mediated international disputes, protected spiritual sites, and attempted to preserve Air Nomad knowledge without turning it into a state-controlled institution.
- Katara has become an internationally respected waterbender and healer while remaining connected to Southern reconstruction.
- Sokka has grown into a strategist, diplomat, inventor, and representative whose nonbending perspective is politically important.
- Toph has refined metalbending and trained selected students, but no later-continuity institution is assumed to exist.
- Suki and the Kyoshi Warriors have expanded their roles in escort, protection, training, and peacekeeping.
- Iroh has served as an informal counselor and White Lotus elder while maintaining a quieter civilian life.
- Azula's legal status, treatment, loyalties, and possible recovery remain unresolved campaign material.
- Ozai remains imprisoned and unable to firebend, but his name still carries symbolic power.

## Historical Consequences at Campaign Start

History should generate present-day conflicts rather than function as decorative lore.

Common consequences include:

- disputed property and inherited claims;
- former collaborators hiding their past;
- veterans unable to adapt to peace;
- missing persons and separated families;
- war crimes investigations;
- resentment toward mixed Fire Nation–Earth Kingdom communities;
- damaged sacred sites and angry spirits;
- propaganda still taught as truth;
- industrial fortunes built on occupation;
- young adults questioning the beliefs of their parents;
- pressure on Aang and Zuko to solve incompatible demands.

The war is over. Its social, moral, and political consequences are not.
