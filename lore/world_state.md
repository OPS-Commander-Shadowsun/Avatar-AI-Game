# World State at Campaign Start

## Date

Year 6 of the Postwar Era, on the anniversary week of the Fire Nation surrender. The exact local calendar may vary by nation.

## Central ages

- Aang: 18 chronologically, excluding the century spent frozen.
- Toph: 18.
- Katara: approximately 20.
- Azula: approximately 20.
- Sokka: approximately 21.
- Zuko: approximately 22.
- Suki, Mai, and Ty Lee: adults in their early twenties.

These ages are game assumptions based on the animated-series age range, not later continuity.

## International situation

The war ended, but peace remains uneven. The Fire Nation has withdrawn forces from many occupied territories while several old settlements remain disputed. Earth Kingdom officials disagree over local autonomy, restitution, and punishment. Refugees still search for homes. Veterans struggle to reintegrate. Smugglers and former officers profit from weak borders.

## Fire Nation

Zuko is the reigning Fire Lord. His reforms have reduced open imperial policy, but the bureaucracy, military, noble houses, and industrial economy were built for war. Loyalists to Ozai operate discreetly. Some citizens consider peace honorable; others see it as humiliation.

## Earth Kingdom

The kingdom is territorially vast and politically fragmented. Ba Sing Se has restored formal royal administration, yet provincial governors, merchant leagues, militias, local rulers, and criminal networks often exercise greater practical control.

## Water Tribes

The Southern Water Tribe is rebuilding population, housing, defenses, and bending knowledge. The Northern Water Tribe possesses greater resources and influence but faces pressure to reform older traditions. Cooperation exists alongside cultural tension.

## Air Nomad legacy

Aang remains the only known living airbender. Air temples are historical, spiritual, and political sites. Pilgrims, scholars, opportunists, and treasure hunters compete over their meaning. Aang is attempting to preserve a culture that cannot be reduced to architecture or costume.

## The Avatar

Aang has become an adult public figure but remains one person with limited time. Communities often expect him to solve conflicts that are political rather than supernatural. His refusal to rule directly frustrates those who want a simple authority.

## Ozai and Azula

Ozai remains imprisoned and powerless as a bender, but his symbolic influence persists. Azula survived the final conflict. Her current treatment and legal status are sensitive state matters. She is neither automatically redeemed nor permanently incapable of change.

## Major campaign pressures

- disputed settlements and property claims;
- veterans, deserters, and war criminals;
- royal legitimacy and regional autonomy;
- spirit disturbances caused by damaged sacred land;
- industrial expansion and environmental harm;
- revenge movements;
- black markets for weapons and military technology;
- preservation versus commercialization of Air Nomad sites;
- intermarriage and mixed cultural identity;
- generational conflict over what peace should mean.
