# Canon Boundary and Divergence Policy

## Purpose

This file defines which facts are binding, which material is excluded, how the six-year bridge period is handled, and how the AI should resolve uncertainty. It exists to prevent accidental import of later continuity and to keep long-running campaigns internally consistent.

## Binding canon

The complete original three-season animated series of *Avatar: The Last Airbender* is binding historical canon.

Binding canon includes:

- events shown or unambiguously established in the animated series;
- characters, relationships, abilities, injuries, affiliations, and deaths established there;
- the known history of the Hundred Year War;
- the existence and broad characteristics of the four nations, the Avatar cycle, bending, spirits, hybrid animals, and the Spirit World;
- Aang's defeat of Ozai through energybending;
- Zuko's coronation as Fire Lord;
- the state of relationships at the end of the animated finale.

The AI may summarize these facts in original language, but must not reproduce episode scripts, transcripts, or lengthy dialogue.

## Excluded material

Do not use information introduced by *The Legend of Korra*.

Do not use post-series comic plots as settled history.

The following are therefore not predetermined:

- future marriages or separations;
- descendants or family lines;
- later cities, states, offices, police forces, schools, companies, movements, or institutions;
- later technological milestones;
- later discoveries about the Avatar cycle or Spirit World;
- later careers or deaths of original-series characters;
- later political settlements involving Fire Nation colonies;
- later forms of bending becoming widespread.

A concept that resembles later continuity may still emerge organically through play, but only if it develops from current campaign events rather than being imported as inevitable history.

## Divergence point

The timeline diverges immediately after Aang defeats Ozai, removes his firebending, and Zuko is crowned Fire Lord.

Everything after that moment belongs to one of three categories:

1. **Repository default** — a conservative assumption needed to begin play.
2. **Campaign fact** — something established during a specific playthrough.
3. **Unresolved possibility** — a future development that must not be treated as already true.

## Original six-year bridge period

The campaign begins six years after the animated finale. These six years are game-original and intentionally conservative.

Default assumptions:

- the Hundred Year War is over;
- no global government has formed;
- Zuko remains Fire Lord;
- Aang serves as an international mediator but does not rule the nations;
- Katara, Sokka, Toph, Zuko, Aang, Suki, Mai, Ty Lee, and Azula are adults;
- reconstruction remains incomplete;
- disputed settlements, refugees, veterans, propaganda, war crimes, economic disruption, and spirit disturbances remain major issues;
- the Fire Nation military has been reduced but not dismantled;
- no later-media political settlement is assumed;
- no canon couple is guaranteed to remain together forever;
- no future family line is fixed;
- no new bending art is common unless established in the series or developed during play;
- technological progress remains gradual and consistent with late-war inventions;
- Azula's condition and legal status remain unresolved and campaign-dependent;
- Ozai remains imprisoned and unable to firebend unless campaign events change this.

## Canon fact versus inference

The AI must label uncertain material internally.

### Canon fact

Directly established by the animated series.

Example: Toph invented metalbending.

### Strong inference

Not stated directly, but highly consistent with canon.

Example: Fire Nation veterans would have difficulty reintegrating after a century of militarization.

### Conservative game assumption

A default needed for play that does not contradict canon.

Example: Zuko has survived several political crises during the six-year bridge.

### Campaign invention

A new person, settlement, faction, law, romance, crime, or event generated for the game.

The narrator may present all four naturally, but must never falsely describe an inference or campaign invention as animated-series canon.

## Character continuity rules

Canon characters retain recognizable identities.

The AI must preserve:

- core values;
- major traumas;
- established loyalties and conflicts;
- known skills and limits;
- speech tendencies and emotional habits;
- existing relationship history;
- moral boundaries unless a long, credible arc changes them.

Adult development is allowed. Characters can mature, change opinions, end relationships, form new relationships, become parents, gain political influence, become corrupted, recover, or die. Such changes require believable causes and must be recorded as campaign continuity.

A single persuasive speech cannot erase years of characterization.

## Relationship continuity

Relationships existing at the end of the series are the default starting state, not permanent destiny.

- Aang and Katara begin as a committed couple.
- Sokka and Suki begin as a committed couple whose duties may keep them apart.
- Zuko and Mai begin with an established romantic history and a potentially complicated current relationship.
- Other romantic possibilities remain open only through adult, consensual, gradual development.

No character is a guaranteed romance option. Existing commitments matter. Attraction does not override autonomy, grief, duty, compatibility, or personal boundaries.

## Ability continuity

Named masters remain formidable.

Rare techniques remain rare:

- energybending is not a normal player upgrade;
- bloodbending is morally and mechanically exceptional;
- lightning generation requires specialized aptitude and training;
- lightning redirection requires correct instruction and timing;
- metalbending requires perception of earthen impurities and specialized practice;
- the Avatar State cannot be used casually without risk;
- spiritual access is not guaranteed by desire alone.

The player may eventually become exceptional, but cannot begin at legendary-master level without an explicit alternate campaign mode.

## Knowledge continuity

Narrator knowledge and character knowledge must remain separate.

A character knows only what they:

- witnessed;
- were told by a credible source;
- learned through their role or studies;
- reasonably inferred;
- discovered during the campaign.

Characters do not know private conversations, distant crimes, hidden loyalties, or secret player actions merely because the narrator knows them.

## Conflict resolution priority

When sources or generated material conflict, use this order:

1. Original animated series.
2. Explicit repository rule.
3. Established campaign continuity.
4. Strong inference from established facts.
5. Conservative game assumption.
6. Random generation.

Lower-priority material must be revised rather than overriding higher-priority material.

## Retcon policy

Avoid retcons whenever possible.

If a contradiction appears:

1. determine whether it is public fact, rumor, mistaken belief, or narrator error;
2. preserve the version already acted upon by the player when feasible;
3. convert minor inconsistencies into in-world uncertainty;
4. openly correct major continuity mistakes in the campaign log;
5. never silently reset relationships, injuries, deaths, pregnancies, political changes, or promises.

## AI enforcement summary

The AI must:

- use only original-series canon as binding source material;
- exclude *The Legend of Korra* and later comic continuity;
- treat the six-year bridge as original and conservative;
- distinguish canon from inference;
- preserve character identity and knowledge limits;
- allow the future to change through play;
- record all significant campaign divergence.
