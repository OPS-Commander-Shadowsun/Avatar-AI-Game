# Airbending

Airbending is the art of guiding air pressure, movement, and momentum through circular footwork, breath, awareness, and freedom of motion. Traditional Air Nomad training treats bending, ethics, spirituality, diet, community, and travel as parts of one discipline rather than separate subjects.

At campaign start, Aang is the only known living airbender. Air Nomad techniques therefore cannot be learned by ordinary player characters unless the campaign deliberately introduces an extraordinary, canon-compatible event. Nonbenders may study Air Nomad philosophy, acrobatics, glider design, meditation, and cultural practice without becoming airbenders.

## How airbending works

An airbender shapes surrounding air into directed currents, pressure changes, cushions, vortices, and bursts of momentum. The element is normally invisible, so results are perceived through dust, clothing, sound, moving objects, temperature, pressure, and bodily impact.

Airbending commonly relies on:

- relaxed but continuous movement;
- circular steps and turns;
- open pathways rather than rigid confrontation;
- rapid perception of space and exits;
- breath control;
- redirection instead of absorbing force;
- using minimal contact to create large changes in balance.

Airbending does not create matter. It manipulates the available atmosphere. Underwater use, sealed environments, smoke-filled rooms, extremely thin air, and cramped spaces significantly change what is possible.

## Core philosophy

Traditional Air Nomad teaching values freedom, detachment, compassion, humor, nonviolence, and respect for life. These values shape combat style: evade, redirect, disarm, separate, escape, and exhaust before inflicting lasting harm.

Philosophy is not a mechanical prohibition. An airbender can injure or kill, especially with falls, pressure, debris, or suffocation. Such use creates severe moral and psychological consequences for a traditionally trained Air Nomad.

## Primary applications

### Mobility

Airbending is exceptionally strong for movement.

- enhanced jumps;
- controlled falls;
- cushioning landings;
- rapid lateral movement;
- wall-assisted movement;
- short bursts of acceleration;
- running across unstable surfaces;
- glider-assisted travel;
- air scooter movement;
- lifting or pushing light objects and people.

Mobility depends on space, awareness, stamina, and safe landing routes.

### Evasion and redirection

Airbenders avoid direct collisions by shifting themselves, opponents, or incoming attacks off line.

- redirect arrows and thrown weapons;
- alter the path of smoke, dust, or flame;
- disrupt a charging opponent's footing;
- push attacks past allies;
- create a brief gap for escape;
- separate combatants without holding ground.

Heavy, anchored, or broad attacks are more difficult to redirect than narrow projectiles.

### Defense

- air shields;
- rotating barriers;
- pressure cushions;
- projectile deflection;
- dispersing smoke or gas;
- slowing falls;
- pushing fire away from civilians;
- softening collisions.

An air shield is not an indestructible wall. Sustained force, surprise, enclosed space, fatigue, or attacks from several directions can break it.

### Offense

- concentrated gusts;
- sweeping knockback;
- pressure strikes;
- vortexes;
- air-assisted debris;
- throwing opponents off balance;
- lifting and dropping objects;
- disrupting breath or stance.

Most traditional applications focus on incapacitation rather than bodily destruction.

### Rescue and disaster response

- clear smoke from buildings;
- create breathing corridors;
- slow falling people or debris;
- move toxic fumes away;
- stabilize ropes, sails, and bridges;
- propel rescue craft;
- ventilate mines or tunnels;
- carry shouted warnings farther under favorable conditions.

### Travel and transport

Air Nomad travel depended on gliders, sky bison, mountain paths, and lightweight equipment. Airbending can assist sails and gliders, but it does not grant unlimited flight without a glider or other support under the campaign's canon rules.

## Signature techniques

### Air scooter

A rotating sphere of compressed air supports rapid ground movement. It requires balance, continuous control, and room to maneuver. Uneven terrain, obstacles, and disrupted concentration can cause a crash.

### Glider-assisted flight

An airbender directs air around a specialized glider to gain lift, steer, climb, descend, and maintain speed. Weather, weight, damage, altitude, and stamina matter. A glider is equipment, not decoration.

### Air shield

A rotating or directed current diverts attacks. It works best against projectiles, flame, dust, and moderate force. Dense stone, sustained water, explosives, and attacks from several directions can overwhelm it.

### Vortex control

Circular motion creates a localized spiral that can trap loose objects, disrupt groups, lift dust, extinguish small fires, or pull targets off balance. Large vortexes require space and substantial stamina.

### Air blade or concentrated pressure

Narrow currents can cut rope, fabric, plants, or weak materials. Using such pressure against living bodies risks severe injury and conflicts with traditional restraint.

### Breath control

Advanced airbenders may warm themselves, regulate breathing, resist altitude, project breath, or use controlled exhalation in close quarters. Breath techniques are not magic immunity to poison, drowning, smoke, or vacuum.

## Subtypes and advanced branches

### Glider arts

Combines airbending with specialized equipment, aerial navigation, weather knowledge, and acrobatics. It includes transport, scouting, rescue, and combat maneuvering.

### Air scooter movement

A high-mobility discipline useful in racing, messenger work, pursuit, escape, and urban traversal. It is difficult to use safely in crowds.

### Vortex and pressure control

Focuses on rotational currents, containment, deflection, and area control. It is useful for firefighting, dust clearing, crowd separation, and combat.

### Sound-related applications

Air can carry, redirect, amplify, or obscure sound within plausible limits. Possible uses include signaling, projecting a warning, creating distracting noise, or reducing the clarity of nearby speech. Airbending does not provide perfect silence or remote eavesdropping.

### Spiritual practice

Meditation, breath, detachment, sacred-site awareness, and Spirit World navigation are associated with Air Nomad tradition. Spiritual skill is not identical to bending power. An airbender can be spiritually blocked, and a nonbender can be spiritually perceptive.

### Extreme pressure or suffocation

Manipulating air around lungs or creating dangerous pressure is theoretically possible but profoundly lethal and morally prohibited in traditional practice. It must never be treated as a casual combat upgrade. Any use requires extreme circumstances and lasting consequences.

## Civilian uses

- ventilation;
- drying crops or clothing;
- controlling smoke in kitchens and workshops;
- assisting sails;
- moving lightweight goods;
- cleaning dust;
- cooling buildings;
- messenger travel;
- festival performances;
- rescue work;
- maintaining mountain routes.

Because only Aang is a known airbender, these services are extremely rare and cannot support widespread industry at campaign start.

## Combat strengths

- exceptional mobility;
- strong evasion;
- nonlethal control;
- projectile defense;
- repositioning allies and enemies;
- effective use of vertical space;
- rapid adaptation;
- strong rescue potential.

## Weaknesses and counters

- narrow or enclosed spaces;
- attacks from several directions;
- opponents anchored to solid terrain;
- wide-area attacks that remove escape routes;
- smoke, poison, or poor air quality;
- underwater combat;
- protecting a fixed location where evasion is insufficient;
- fatigue from constant movement;
- emotional fixation that reduces flexibility;
- restraints or injury that disrupt circular movement.

## Training

Traditional progression includes:

1. breathing and balance;
2. footwork and evasion;
3. controlled gusts;
4. falling safely;
5. staff and glider handling;
6. air scooter practice;
7. shields and redirection;
8. vortex control;
9. rescue applications;
10. ethical restraint and spiritual integration.

Air Nomad mastery tattoos represent genuine mastery and cultural recognition, not simply a power score.

## AI simulation rules

- Air currents are usually invisible; describe their effects.
- Preserve the difference between evasion and invulnerability.
- Do not grant unsupported unaided flight.
- Do not make Aang omnipresent merely because air is everywhere.
- Apply room size, oxygen, smoke, weather, altitude, and landing space.
- Airbending can be lethal, but Aang's moral boundaries remain important.
- Do not create ordinary new airbenders without a major campaign decision.
- Do not import later-era airbending institutions or abilities.
