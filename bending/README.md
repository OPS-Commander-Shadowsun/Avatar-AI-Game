# Bending Reference Index

This directory defines the physical, cultural, ethical, and gameplay rules for bending in the postwar simulation.

## Required loading order

1. [Core Bending Rules](core_rules.md)
2. Load the relevant elemental chapter:
   - [Airbending](air.md)
   - [Waterbending](water.md)
   - [Earthbending](earth.md)
   - [Firebending](fire.md)
3. Load the active character profile for personal style, willingness, known techniques, and stat values.
4. Apply current campaign state, injuries, environment, equipment, moon phase, weather, and fatigue.

## Element and subtype map

### Air

- mobility and evasion
- glider arts
- air scooter movement
- vortex and pressure control
- sound-related applications
- breath and spiritual practice

### Water

- liquid water control
- icebending
- healing
- mist, fog, and steam management
- plantbending
- bloodbending

### Earth

- stone and soil manipulation
- seismic sense
- sandbending
- metalbending
- crystal, coal, ore, and mineral control
- mud interaction
- exceptionally rare lavabending

### Fire

- flame generation
- heat control
- fire propulsion
- lightning generation
- lightning redirection
- blue fire as an individual expression
- exceptionally rare combustion-based techniques

### Avatar-exclusive or exceptional

- command of all four elements
- Avatar State
- energybending
- contact with past Avatars under appropriate spiritual conditions

## Runtime principles

- A technique being physically possible does not mean the active character knows it.
- A character sheet overrides generic assumptions about personal mastery.
- Rare subtypes require documented aptitude, teaching, discovery, or campaign events.
- Bending Power is separate from core attributes and does not guarantee success.
- Terrain, material, stamina, injury, emotion, collateral damage, and ethical boundaries always matter.
- Later-era developments from *The Legend of Korra* are excluded.
